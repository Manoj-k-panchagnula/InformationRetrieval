	<h1>
		Ladybird Johnson Wildflower Garden Workday</h1>

	<h1>
		May 6, 9 AM - 1 PM</h1>

	<h1>
		<strong>100+ Volunteers Needed!</strong></h1>

	<h1>
		&nbsp;</h1>
</div>

<p class="text_content">
	The Ladybird Johnson Memorial Wildflower Garden is in need of some TLC, and we need you to help with the revitalization effort! &nbsp;The garden, located near the Waranch Tennis Complex, was dedicated in 2010, and is in desperate need of some care and attention. &nbsp;The Ladybird Johnson Memorial Wildflower Garden&rsquo;s purpose is to provide a habitat of native plants and grasses that are beneficial to pollinators, such as butterflies, moths and bees.&nbsp;<br />
	<br />
	The goal of this workday will be to get 225 perennials into the ground at the LBJ Memorial Wildflower garden and to extend the rock border at the site.&nbsp;&nbsp;The revitalized garden will serve as a sanctuary for pollinators and migrating monarchs as well as a&nbsp;resource for providing education and outreach about the important role pollinators play in our ecosystem.&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />
	<br />
	To those&nbsp;joining us on May 6, we will provide work gloves, garden tools and water to fill your refillable water bottles. <strong>Please contact the Sustainability Coordinator, Gary&nbsp;Cocke, with any questions at <a href="mailto:Gary.Cocke@unt.edu">gary.cocke@unt.edu</a>.</strong></p>
