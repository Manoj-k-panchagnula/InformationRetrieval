<p style="margin-top: 30px;">The <span class="blue">UNT Office for Nationally Competitive Scholarship</span> (formerly the Office of Postgraduate Fellowships) assists undergraduate and graduate students in identifying and pursuing externally funded research and study opportunities. <a href="http://www.opgf.unt.edu/index.htm#duban">Dr. James Duban</a>, director of the office, invites students to inquire about the preparation and credentials necessary to compete successfully for such postgraduate awards as <a href="http://www.opgf.unt.edu/postgrad.htm#rhodes">Rhodes</a>, <a href="http://www.opgf.unt.edu/postgrad.htm#marshall">Marshall</a>, <a href="http://www.opgf.unt.edu/postgrad.htm#fulbright">Fulbright</a>, <a href="http://www.opgf.unt.edu/postgrad.htm#madison">James Madison</a>, <a href="http://www.opgf.unt.edu/postgrad.htm#truman">Harry S. Truman</a>, <a href="http://www.opgf.unt.edu/postgrad.htm#mellon">Andrew W. Mellon</a>, and <a href="http://www.opgf.unt.edu/postgrad.htm#nsf">National Science Foundation</a> scholarships and fellowships.</p>

<p>This site describes the scholarships that are generally considered the most prestigious. Many other scholarships are available, and information about those will be provided upon request from this office.</p>

<p>Even freshmen and sophomores should begin thinking about postgraduate prospects. This site describes four undergraduate scholarships: the <a href="http://www.opgf.unt.edu/distinct.htm#goldwater">Barry M. Goldwater Scholarship</a>, the <a href="http://www.opgf.unt.edu/distinct.htm#udall">Morris K. Udall Scholarship</a>, the <a href="http://www.opgf.unt.edu/distinct.htm#rotary">Rotary Ambassadorial Scholarships</a>, and the <a href="http://www.opgf.unt.edu/distinct.htm#mcnair">Ronald E. McNair Post-Baccalaureate Achievement Scholarship</a>. The awards are intrinsically worth pursuing and have the added benefit of enhancing a student's prospects for receiving a postgraduate fellowship. Note, as well, that the <a href="postgrad.htm#truman">Harry S. Truman Scholarship</a>, awarded to college juniors, funds a senior year of study and two-to-three years of graduate study.</p>

<p>Applying for a national scholarship is a &quot;no lose&quot; situation, since undergraduates are able to channel their essays into conventional graduate school applications. They thereby enhance their prospects for being accepted into a graduate program and for receiving internal fellowship support. Graduate students, in turn, often end up formulating a dissertation proposal from the writing which they undertake as part of the process of applying for a national scholarship.</p>

<p>Most postgraduate fellowship <span class="red">deadlines</span> occur from <span class="blue">September through November</span>. <span class="red">Serious contenders should begin researching, writing, and organizing their applications in the spring and summer preceding fall deadlines.</span> In some cases, such as with <a href=="http://www.opgf.unt.edu/postgrad.htm#marshall">Marshall</a>, <a href=="http://www.opgf.unt.edu/postgrad.htm#rhodes">Rhodes</a>, and <a href="http://www.opgf.unt.edu/postgrad.htm#truman">Truman</a> scholarships, <a href=="http://www.unt.edu/">UNT</a> faculty screening committees read applications and invite the most promising candidates to on-campus interviews. The screening committees then decide whom to nominate to represent <a href="http://www.unt.edu/">UNT</a> at state and regional levels of competition.</p>

<p>Finalists interact intensively and regularly with these committees to prepare for off-campus interviews.</p>
