<p style="text-align: left;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">The Hazlewood Act is a State of Texas benefit that provides qualified  Veterans, spouses, and dependent children with an education benefit of  up to 150 hours of tuition exemption, including most fee charges, at  public institutions of higher education in Texas. This does NOT include  living expenses, books, or supply fees.</p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><a name="veteran"></a><strong><span style="font-size: large;">Veteran<span style="font-size: large;"> </span>Qualifications&nbsp;</span></strong></p>
<p>A Veteran must:</p>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>At  the time of entry into active duty the U.S. Armed Forces, designated  Texas as Home of Record; or entered the service in Texas; or was a Texas  resident;</li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Have  received an honorable discharge or separation or a general discharge  under honorable conditions as indicated on the Veteran's Certificate of  Release or Discharge from Active Duty (DD Form 214);</li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Served at least 181 days of active duty service (excluding training);</li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Have  no federal Veteran&rsquo;s education benefits, or have no federal Veterans  education benefits dedicated to the payment of tuition and fees only  (such as Chapter 33 or 31; for term or semester enrolled that do not  exceed the value of Hazlewood benefits;</li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Not be in default on a student loan made or guaranteed by the State of Texas; </li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Enroll  in classes for which the college receives tax support (i.e., a course  that does not depend solely on student tuition and fees to cover its  cost), unless the college&rsquo;s governing board has ruled to let Veterans  receive the benefit while taking non-funded courses; and</li>
<li>Meet the GPA requirement of the institution's  satisfactory academic   progress policy in a degree or certificate  program as determined by  the  institution's financial aid policy and, as  an undergraduate  student, not be considered to have attempted an  excessive amount of  credit hours. </li>
</ul>
<p>Veterans who are granted their first Hazlewood Act exemption  beginning fall, 2011 must reside in Texas during the semester or term  for which the exemption is claimed. This requirement does not apply to  the Veterans who either received the exemption prior to the 2011-2012  academic year, have reenlisted into active duty, or reside with a spouse  who is on active duty.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><strong><span style="font-size: large;">Hazlewood Act application process<br /></span></strong></p>
<p class="MsoListParagraphCxSpFirst">A Veteran must:</p>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Apply and be accepted to a <strong>Texas public</strong> college or university of his/her choice. Go to<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span><a href="http://www.applytexas.org">www.applytexas.org</a> to apply or use your institution's application for admission;</li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Provide proof (DD214) from the Department of Defense regarding military service and the nature of discharge;</li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Provide  proof of eligibility or ineligibility for GI Bill benefits (Chapter 31,  33/Post-9/11) by requesting a certificate of eligibility for federal  education benefits from <a href="https://myaccess.dmdc.osd.mil/identitymanagement/authenticate.do;jsessionid=9MkbQzwY9y3LFjhcXMYSKuAIbxtdFtZNgGd7XXAEkX4nj9rPxhb7%21132329868?execution=e1s1">eBenefits</a> (if Veteran has active duty service after 9/11/2001). You will need to  create a username and password to request your certificate of  eligibility;<br /></li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Fill out the Hazlewood Exemption application form; and <br /></li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Turn  in the Hazlewood Exemption application form, a copy of your letter of  eligibility/ineligibility, and a copy of your DD214 into the financial  aid office of the institution you will be attending.</li>
</ul>
<p>Applications and all supporting documentation must be received by the  institution no later than the last day of class in order to be  evaluated for the semester or term.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><strong><span style="font-size: large;">Determine institution eligibility for the Hazlewood Act <br /></span></strong></p>
<p class="MsoNormal">Eligible Institutions:<br /> The Hazlewood Act is   available only for use at a Texas public college or university. To   access listings of Texas public colleges and universities, follow this   link: <a href="http://www.collegeforalltexans.com/index.cfm?ObjectID=D57D0AC5-AB2D-EFB0-FC201080B528442A">Texas Institutions of Higher Education</a> and select any of the schools listed under the Texas Public Institutions. The schools listed under <strong>Independent (Private) Institutions </strong>do <strong>NOT </strong>qualify for this exemption.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><a name="Legacy Act"></a><strong><span style="font-size: large;">Legacy Act (Child)</span></strong></p>
<p class="MsoNormal">Eligible Veterans may assign unused hours of exemption eligibility to a child under certain conditions.</p>
<p><strong>Qualifications</strong><br />A child (legacy recipient) must:&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Be classified by the institution as a Texas resident;</li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Be the biological child, stepchild, adopted child, or claimed as a dependent in the current or previous tax<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> </span>year;</li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Be   25 years old or younger on the first day of the semester or term for   which the exemption is claimed (unless granted an extension due to a   qualifying illness or debilitating condition); and</li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Meet  the GPA requirement of the institution's satisfactory academic   progress policy in a degree or certificate program as determined by the   institution's financial aid policy and, as an undergraduate student,  not be considered to have attempted an excessive amount of credit hours.<br /></li>
</ul>
<p class="MsoNormal">Legacy recipients will receive an exemption for the  number of degree certified hours reported by the institution for that  term or semester. Maximum degree certified hours awarded to the Legacy  recipient will be dependent upon the degree or certificate program in  which the student is enrolled for that term or semester and shall be  consistent with the program length as defined within the school catalog  as approved by the regional accreditation commission. *If a child to  whom hours have been delegated fails to use all of the assigned hours, a  Veteran may re-assign the unused hours that are available to another  child. Only one child will use Hazlewood Legacy benefits at a time.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><strong><span style="font-size: large;">Legacy Act Application Process <br /></span></strong></p>
<p><strong>Qualifications</strong><br />A child (legacy recipient) of a Veteran must:</p>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Apply and be accepted to a Texas public college or university. Go to <a href="http://www.applytexas.org">www.applytexas.org</a> to apply or use your institution's application for admission; <br /></li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Fill out the Hazlewood Exemption Application;</li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Provide   proof of eligibility or ineligibility for GI Bill benefits  (Chapter  31, 33/Post-9/11) by requesting a certificate of eligibility for federal  education benefits from <a href="https://myaccess.dmdc.osd.mil/identitymanagement/authenticate.do;jsessionid=9MkbQzwY9y3LFjhcXMYSKuAIbxtdFtZNgGd7XXAEkX4nj9rPxhb7%21132329868?execution=e1s1">eBenefits</a>. You will need to  create a username and password to request your certificate of  eligibility; <br /></li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Take   both applications, letter of eligibility/ineligibility (if needed),   along with a copy of the Veteran&rsquo;s DD214, to the financial aid office of   the institution you will be attending.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span></li>
</ul>
<p>Applications and all supporting documentation must be received by the  institution no later than the last day of class of a term in order to  be evaluated for that semester or term.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><a name="spouse/child hazlewood"></a><strong><span style="font-size: large;">Hazlewood Act for Spouse/Child</span></strong></p>
<p class="MsoNormal">Spouses and dependent children of eligible Active  Duty, Reserve, and Texas National Guard who died in the line of duty or  as a result of injury or illness directly related to military service,  are missing in action, or who became totally disabled for purposes of  employability as a result of a service-related injury or illness are  entitled to each receive a 150 credit hours exemption.</p>
<p><strong>Qualifications</strong></p>
<p>A spouse must:</p>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Be  a spouse of a Veteran who, at the time of entry into the U.S. Armed  Forces, be classified by the institution as a Texas resident, designated  Texas as Home of Record, or entered the service in Texas;</li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Be  a spouse of a Veteran of the U.S. Armed Forces or the Texas National  Guard who died as a result of service-related injuries or illness, is  missing in action, or became totally disabled (100%) as a result of  service-related injury or illness or is entitled to receive compensation  at the 100% rate due to individual employability (IU) due to a service  connected injury or illness;<br /></li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Have  no federal Veterans education benefits, or have no federal Veterans  education benefits dedicated to the payment of tuition and fees only  (such as Chapter 33 or 31) for term or semester enrolled that do not  exceed the value of Hazlewood benefits; <br /></li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Be classified by the institution as a Texas resident; and<br /></li>
<li>Meet the GPA requirement of the institution's  satisfactory academic   progress policy in a degree or certificate  program as determined by  the  institution's financial aid policy and, as  an undergraduate  student, not be considered to have attempted an  excessive amount of  credit hours.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> This requirement does not apply to the spouse of a MIA, KIA, or service connected deceased Veteran. <br /></span></li>
</ul>
<p class="MsoNormal">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">A child must:</p>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Be  a child of a Veteran who, at the time of entry into the U.S. Armed  Forces, be classified by the institution as a Texas resident, designated  Texas as Home of Record, or entered the service in Texas;</li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Be  a child of a Veteran of the U.S. Armed Forces, Texas National Guard, or  Texas Air National Guard who died as a result of service-related  injuries or illness, is missing in action, or became totally disabled  (100%) as a result of a service-related injury or illness or is entitled  to receive compensation at the 100% rate due to individual  employability (IU) due to a service connected injury or illness;</li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Have  no federal Veterans education benefits, or have no federal Veterans  education benefits dedicated to the payment of tuition and fees only  (such as Chapter 33 or 31; for term or semester enrolled that do not  exceed the value of Hazlewood benefits; </li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Be classified by the institution as a Texas resident; and</li>
<li>Meet the GPA requirement of the institution's  satisfactory academic   progress policy in a degree or certificate  program as determined by  the  institution's financial aid policy and, as  an undergraduate  student, not be considered to have attempted an  excessive amount of  credit hours.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> This requirement does not apply to the child of a MIA, KIA, or service connected deceased Veteran. </span></li>
</ul>
<p class="MsoNormal">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><strong><span style="font-size: large;">Application process for Spouse/Child </span></strong></p>
<p class="MsoNormal">A spouse or child of a Veteran whose death was service connected, is MIA, or is/was 100% permanently disabled must:</p>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Apply and be accepted to a Texas public college or university. Go to <a href="http://www.applytexas.org">www.applytexas.org</a> or use your institution's application for admission; </li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Provide a DD214 and a disability rating letter or a DD1300 Report of Casualty regarding the Veteran's death; </li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Provide  proof of eligibility or ineligibility for GI Bill benefits (Chapter 31,  33/Post/9-11) by requesting an education benefits letter from the VA at  <a href="https://myaccess.dmdc.osd.mil/identitymanagement/authenticate.do;jsessionid=9MkbQzwY9y3LFjhcXMYSKuAIbxtdFtZNgGd7XXAEkX4nj9rPxhb7%21132329868?execution=e1s1">eBenefits</a>. You will need to  create a username and password to request your certificate of  eligibility;<br /></li>
<li><span style="font-family: Symbol;"><span style="mso-list: Ignore;"></span></span>Fill out the Hazlewood Exemption application form.</li>
</ul>
<p>Applications and all supporting documentation must be received by the  institution no later than the last day of class of a term in order to  be evaluated for that semester or term.</p>
