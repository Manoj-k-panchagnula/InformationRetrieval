<h2 style="text-align:center">University of North Texas Bulletin | 2015-2016 Graduate Catalog</h2>

<h5 style="text-align:center">Official release date is July 1, 2015<br />
Catalog goes into effect at the beginning of the 2015 fall semester<br />
Program requirements are subject to necessary corrections</h5>

<hr />
<p style="text-align:center">&#160;<img alt="" src="/mime/media/14/1527/Home Page Image.jpg" style="height:337px; width:739px" /></p>

<p>This catalog is an official bulletin of the University of North Texas and is intended to provide general information. It includes policies, regulations, procedures and fees in effect at the time of release. UNT reserves the right to make changes at any time to reflect current Board of Regents rules, university administrative policies and procedures, changes in federal or state law, and fee changes. Information provided in this catalog is subject to change without notice and does not constitute a contract between the University of North Texas and a student, an applicant for admission, or other individual. Updated policies and administrative procedures can be found on departmental or university web sites.</p>

<p>Students are responsible for observing the policies included here; therefore, they are urged to read this catalog carefully. This catalog does not include all university policies and procedures for which students are responsible. Students also should consult other publications, such as the <em>Student Handbook</em>, the <em>Code of Student Conduct</em>, <em>Parking Regulations</em>, <em>Housing Handbook </em>and specific contracts. This catalog becomes effective on the first day of the fall term/semester, 2015.</p>

<h3>Non-Discrimination Policy</h3>

<p>It is the policy of the University of North Texas not to discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, age, national origin, disability, disabled veteran status or veterans of the Vietnam era status in its educational programs, activities, admissions or employment policies.</p>

<p>The university complies with federal and state equal opportunity laws and regulations, and through its diversity policy declares harassment, which is based on individual differences (including sexual orientation), to be inconsistent with the university's mission and educational goals.</p>
