              <div class="breadcrumb"><a href="/">Home</a> » <a href="/registration">Registration</a> » <a href="/nolink/966">Before</a> » <a href="/registration/get-advised" class="active">Get Advised</a></div><h2>Get Advised</h2><div id="node-89" class="node clear-block">



  <div class="meta">
  
    </div>

  <div class="content">
    <p>Advising is available prior to and during registration for continuing students. You should contact the office of your advisor and schedule an appointment one to two weeks prior to your scheduled registration time. It is strongly recommended that all students see their advisors prior to the beginning of registration. Advisors provide the following:</p>
<ul>
<li>Help with course selection</li>
<li>Graduation checks</li>
<li>Degree audits</li>
<li>Referrals to campus resources</li>
</ul>
<p>For more information, visit <a href="http://advising.unt.edu/">advising.unt.edu</a>.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong style="font-family: inherit; font-style: inherit; line-height: 1.5;">Advising is mandatory for ALL students on academic probation</strong><span style="font-size: 12px; line-height: 1.5;">. If you are on academic probation, or if the department of your major requires that you be advised before you register, then the registration system will ask that you enter an advising code before you will be allowed to schedule classes.</span></p>
<p><strong>Advising procedures vary by college</strong>. Contact your advising office for more information. Call at least one week ahead of your registration date to schedule your appointment</p>
<p><strong>Graduate students</strong> must comply with departmental requirements for advising. Contact the advising office of your major for specific instructions. Check&nbsp;<a href="http://tsgs.unt.edu/academics/advisors">http://tsgs.unt.edu/academics/advisors</a>&nbsp;for more information.</p>
<h3>Orientation and Advising for New Undergraduate Students</h3>
<p>All new undergraduate students must attend orientation and are required to have an advising code in order to register.</p>
<p>For more information about new student orientation, visit <a href="http://transition.unt.edu/orientation">transition.unt.edu/orientation</a> or contact the Office of Orientation and Transition Programs at 940-565-4198 or 940-369-8397.</p>

<h3><strong>Course Permission Numbers</strong></h3>
<p><a name="permission"></a>If you are registering for restricted courses, special problems, internships, or any course with variable credit, you must obtain the course permission number prior to registering for those courses.</p>
<p>In addition, your course selection may be restricted either due to state or university policy or departmental advising. Check with the department of your major to find out if there are any restrictions on your enrollment.</p>
<h4><strong>Federation Course Registration</strong></h4>
<p>UNT students wishing to enroll in <strong>graduate courses</strong> at Texas A&amp;M University--Commerce or Texas Woman’s University under the cooperative enrollment program of the Federation of North Texas Area Universities should first secure approval, and in some departments an approval form, from their major advisor for the specific courses. The course title appearing on the UNT academic transcript will be identical to that of the course as offered by the other institution. Class schedules for both TAMU-C and TWU are available online. Students can find more information, as well as the form to enroll in a Federation course, on the <a href="http://tsgs.unt.edu/new-current-students/federation-north-texas-universities-cross-registration">UNT Federation website</a> or by contacting the UNT Federation Representative at <a href="mailto:Jackie.Karlsson@unt.edu">Jackie.Karlsson@unt.edu</a> or 940-565-4600.</p>
<h4><strong>Special Notice to Graduate Students</strong></h4>
<p>You may have been provisionally admitted during the current semester without the required standardized test (such as the GRE). However, you will not be allowed to register for future semesters unless you have a satisfactory test score on file in the Toulouse Graduate School. Only official score reports from the Educational Testing Service are acceptable.&nbsp;</p>
<h3>Additional Resources</h3>
<p>For more information, contact the Registrar's Information Counter by calling 940-565-2111 or visiting Eagle Student Services Center (ESSC) Room 147.</p>
<p>For instructions on using the EIS Student Center for Registration, please go to the Help for Students page at: <a href="http://essc.unt.edu/eis/students.htm">essc.unt.edu/eis/students.htm</a></p>
