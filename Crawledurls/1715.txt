    <div class="faq-question">
    <a href="/faq/how-do-i-find-policy" id="n894">How do I find a policy?</a>  </div> <!-- Close div: faq-question -->

  <div class="faq-answer">
    <p>Specific policies may be found a number of ways. Options include:</p>
<ul>
    <li class="rteleft">Browsing by policy number (<em>this</em> <em>may be most&nbsp;helpful&nbsp;until you&nbsp;get familiar&nbsp;with the new site</em>);</li>
    <li class="rteleft">Entering a keyword in the Search;</li>
    <li>Browsing by chapter <em>(e.g., Faculty Affairs, Human Resources); </em></li>
    <li>Browsing the full alphabetical list of policies; or&nbsp;</li>
    <li>Browsing by policy owner or by contact person (<em>this helps&nbsp;owners&nbsp;and contact persons keep track of their policies</em> and<em>&nbsp;informs&nbsp;members of the UNT Community&nbsp;where to direct questions or comments</em>).</li>
</ul>
<p>If you still need assistance in finding a policy, you can contact the UNT Policy&nbsp;Specialist at (940) 565-4535 or e-mail <a href="mailto:valerie.robertson@unt.edu">deena.merrill@unt.edu</a>.</p>    </div> <!-- Close div: faq-answer -->
        <div class="faq-question">
    <a href="/faq/how-do-i-know-if-policy-was-recently-updated" id="n893">How do I know if a policy was recently updated? </a>  </div> <!-- Close div: faq-question -->

  <div class="faq-answer">
    <p>The <a href="http://policy.unt.edu/disposition">Disposition Table</a>&nbsp;is updated every time there is a substantive policy change. The Disposition Table&nbsp;provides a listing of policies that have been deleted or&nbsp;substantively revised, including the reason the policy for the substantive policy change.</p>    </div> <!-- Close div: faq-answer -->
        <div class="faq-question">
    <a href="/faq/id-post-policy-our-departmental-web-page-are-there-any-rules-about" id="n148">I&#039;d like to post a policy on our departmental web page. Are there any rules about this?</a>  </div> <!-- Close div: faq-question -->

  <div class="faq-answer">
    <p>Only one version of a University-wide policy may exist and that version resides on the official policies page. This is to ensure proper updating of policies, and consistency in presentation. Any policies referenced on a department web page should link back to the official web page. In other words, do not cut, copy, and paste the policy onto the departmental page, but link to the policy that resides within the University Policy Manual&nbsp;on the official policy page.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>    </div> <!-- Close div: faq-answer -->
        <div class="faq-question">
    <a href="/faq/when-will-policies-be-reviewed" id="n144">When will policies be reviewed? </a>  </div> <!-- Close div: faq-question -->

  <div class="faq-answer">
    <p>The review process is a huge undertaking and will be an ongoing effort. The UNT Policy&nbsp;Specialist has developed a timeline for the transition of all policies&nbsp;and&nbsp;is&nbsp;working with&nbsp;each policy proponent/owner to&nbsp;guide the process. The Policy Advisory Group continues to meet and make progress. The scheduled timeline does not preclude revising policies as the need arises.&nbsp;</p>    </div> <!-- Close div: faq-answer -->
        <div class="faq-question">
    <a href="/faq/what-if-i-only-need-make-minor-revisions-or-non-substantive-changes-policy" id="n143">What if I only need to make minor revisions or non-substantive changes to a policy?</a>  </div> <!-- Close div: faq-question -->

  <div class="faq-answer">
    <div class="rteleft">Non-substantive policy changes are changes that primarily pertain to technical revisions such as changes to position titles, words, definitions, procedures, grammar corrections, etc.<em> (i.e. - changing the number of people on a committee from four to five.)&nbsp;&nbsp;</em><strong>Note:<em> </em></strong>All non-substantive changes are subject to approval by the UNT&nbsp;System Office of General Counsel.</div>
<ol>
    <li>Contact the UNT Policy&nbsp;Specialist who will assist in determining whether the change is substantive or&nbsp; non-substantive.</li>
    <li>Complete the Policy Approval Form, attach the revised policy and obtain the appropriate signatures.</li>
    <li>Submit a hardcopy and an electronic version of the policy draft&nbsp;to the UNT Policy Specialist, along with a redline version of the proposed changes.</li>
    <li>Non-substantive changes will be made to the policy website.&nbsp;</li>
</ol>
<p>&nbsp;</p>    </div> <!-- Close div: faq-answer -->
        <div class="faq-question">
    <a href="/faq/what-if-i-need-make-substantive-changes-policy" id="n141">What if I need to make substantive changes to a policy?</a>  </div> <!-- Close div: faq-question -->

  <div class="faq-answer">
    <p>Substantive policy changes are changes that: 1) affect the University&rsquo;s legal, regulatory, financial and ethical obligations, responsibilities, or 2) affect&nbsp;the way the University&rsquo;s business is conducted in support of mission-critical operations impacting the roles of various departments across the University, or 3) affect&nbsp;the actions required by a targeted audience to remain in compliance with the policy provisions.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>To make a substantive change to a policy, you should:</p>
<ol>
    <li>Contact the UNT Policy&nbsp;Specialist who will assist in determining whether the proposed change is substantive or non-substantive.</li>
    <li>Complete the Administrative Policy Planning Worksheet and submit it to the UNT Policy Specialist.</li>
    <li>Transfer the policy to the UNT Policy Template and highlight the substantive changes. Submit an electronic version of the policy to the UNT Policy Specialist.</li>
    <li>Policy Proponent/owner of the policy will present the policy to the Policy Advisory Group.</li>
    <li>PAG will review, discuss and determine the final version of the policy. (Note: Some recommended changes to existing policies may go to the President or the President&rsquo;s Cabinet for approval or disapproval.)</li>
    <li>The UNT Policy&nbsp;Specialist will update the UNT policy website.&nbsp;</li>
</ol>    </div> <!-- Close div: faq-answer -->
        <div class="faq-question">
    <a href="/faq/how-do-i-create-new-policy" id="n138">How do I create a new policy? </a>  </div> <!-- Close div: faq-question -->

  <div class="faq-answer">
    <ol>
    <li>Complete the Administrative Policy Planning Worksheet and UNT Policy Template, obtain the necessary approvals and then submit to the UNT Policy Specialist.</li>
    <li>Policy Proponent/owner of the policy will present the policy to&nbsp;the Policy Advisory&nbsp;Group.&nbsp;</li>
    <li>Policy Advisory Group&nbsp;will review, discuss and make a determination on the final version of the policy. (Note: All new policies will require approval from the President.) &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;</li>
</ol>    </div> <!-- Close div: faq-answer -->
        <div class="faq-question">
    <a href="/faq/there-new-format-university-policies" id="n137">Is there a new format for University policies?</a>  </div> <!-- Close div: faq-question -->

  <div class="faq-answer">
    <p>Yes. All policies will be transferred to the new policy template by May 2011. This template, created by the Office of General Counsel, will provide uniformity to all policies. The UNT Policy&nbsp;Specialist is also available to assist in understanding how to use the new template.&nbsp;&nbsp;You may access a copy of the template in the <em>Forms&nbsp;&amp; Tools</em><strong> </strong>section of the policy website.</p>    </div> <!-- Close div: faq-answer -->
        <div class="faq-question">
    <a href="/faq/who-will-be-reviewing-policies" id="n136">Who will be reviewing the policies?</a>  </div> <!-- Close div: faq-question -->

  <div class="faq-answer">
    <p>The Policy Advisory Group (PAG), chaired by the UNT Policy Specialist, was established in July 2008 for the sole purpose of reviewing substantive policies.&nbsp; This group consists of leadership representation from key departments including Academic Affairs, Student Affairs, Finance/Controller, Research and Economic Development, Information Technology, Institutional Equity and Diversity, UNT Policy Department, and Human Resources.</p>
<p>In addition to the PAG, policies are reviewed by the Office of General Counsel and key stakeholders across the campus community, when appropriate, such as the Faculty Senate, Staff Senate, and others.</p>    </div> <!-- Close div: faq-answer -->
        <div class="faq-question">
    <a href="/faq/what-policy-review-project" id="n134">What is the Policy Review Project?</a>  </div> <!-- Close div: faq-question -->

  <div class="faq-answer">
    <p>&nbsp;The president&nbsp;authorized a comprehensive review and where necessary, revision of the entire UNT Policy Manual to ensure the University is operating with a clear, concise, up-to-date set of policies that reflect the most fair and efficient practices as well as the UNT System's Regents Rules.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span></span></p>    </div> <!-- Close div: faq-answer -->
