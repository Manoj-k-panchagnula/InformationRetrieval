<P><FONT SIZE=+1>The </FONT><B><FONT COLOR="#FF0000" SIZE=+1>Center for
Environmental Philosophy</FONT></B><FONT SIZE=+1> was created by the board
of directors of </FONT><B><FONT COLOR="#FF0000" SIZE=+1>Environmental Philosophy,
Inc.</FONT></B><FONT SIZE=+1>, a nonprofit corporation of the State of New
Mexico. It is the product of a number of activities begun with the creation
of the journal <A HREF="/enethics.html">Environmental Ethics.</A></FONT></P>


<P><FONT SIZE=+1>The journal was founded in 1978 by <A HREF="http://www.cep.unt.edu/vech.html">Eugene
C. Hargrove</A> at the <A HREF="http://www.unm.edu/">University of New Mexico</A>
in Albuquerque. The first issue was published in January 1979. In September
1980 a nonprofit organization, </FONT><B><FONT COLOR="#FF0000" SIZE=+1>Environmental
Philosophy, Inc.</FONT></B><FONT SIZE=+1>, was created to be the owner of
the journal. It is a 501(c)3 tax-exempt organization created for educational,
scientific, and literary purposes.</FONT></P>


<P><FONT SIZE=+1>The journal moved to the <A HREF="http://philserv.phil.uga.edu/">University
of Georgia</A> in fall 1981. The </FONT><B><FONT COLOR="#FF0000" SIZE=+1>Center
for Environmental Philosophy</FONT></B><FONT SIZE=+1> was established in
1989 by </FONT><B><FONT COLOR="#FF0000" SIZE=+1>Environmental Philosophy,
Inc.</FONT></B><FONT SIZE=+1> as a center for its various activities in
publishing, research, and education.</FONT></P>


<P><FONT SIZE=+1>The center moved to the <A HREF="http://www.unt.edu/">University
of North Texas</A> in 1990 and was given the status of an affiliated organization
in 1991 by the </FONT><B><FONT COLOR="#FF0000" SIZE=+1>UNT Council of Deans.</FONT></B></P>


<p><FONT SIZE=+1>The center moved to its current location in the newly built 
<a href="http://www.cep.unt.edu/eesat.html">Environmental Education, Science, 
and Technology Building</a> in April 1998 (pictured above) together with the 
<a href="http://www.ias.unt.edu/">Institute of Applied Sciences</a>, the 
<a href="http://www.phil.unt.edu/">Department of Philosophy and Religion Studies</a>, 
the 
 <a href="http://www.geog.unt.edu/">Department of Geography</a>,   and the 
<a href="http://www.efec.unt.edu">Elm Fork Education Center</a>.</font>

<P><FONT SIZE=+1>The primary activities of the center are currently:</FONT></P>


<UL>
  <LI><FONT SIZE=+1>the publishing of the journal <A HREF="/enethics.html">Environmental
  Ethics </A></FONT>
  <LI><FONT SIZE=+1>the reprinting of important books through its book series
  <A HREF="/eebooks.html">Environmental Ethics Books</A></FONT>
  <LI><FONT SIZE=+1>training and the promotion of research in environmental
  ethics through <A HREF="/work.html">workshops</A> and <A HREF="/conf.html">conferences</A>.</FONT>
  <LI><FONT SIZE=+1>and the promotion of <A HREF="http://www.phil.unt.edu/graduate.htm">graduate
  education </A>in the field of environmental ethics as well as <A HREF=
  "http://www.phil.unt.edu/postdoc.htm">postdoctoral research and professional
  development</A> in cooperation with the <A HREF="http://www.phil.unt.edu/">Department
  of Philosophy and Religion Studies</A> at the <A HREF="http://www.unt.edu/">University
  of North Texas</A>.</FONT>
</UL>
