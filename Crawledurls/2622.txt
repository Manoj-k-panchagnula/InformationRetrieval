        <blockquote>
        <p>The University of North Texas conducted a routine test of its Eagle Alert  system <strong>April 22.</strong> The  system is tested once each semester.</p>
<p>UNT rolled out Eagle  Alert to notify campus with immediate emergency information in fall 2007.</p>
<p>Eagle  Alert allows UNT administrators to quickly contact campus community members by  phone about emergency situations affecting the safety and well-being of people on  and around the UNT campus. </p>
<p>The system sends recorded voice messages to the  phones, including cell phones, of all active faculty, staff and students. The  system also can send text messages to cell phones if the cell phone user has  granted the system permission to do so. <br />
  <br />
  Eagle Alert only will be used for critical situations, including:</p>
<ul>
  <li>campus closings because of  inclement weather</li>
  <li>public safety emergencies  like chemical spills, fires or violence</li>
</ul>
<p>Messages will not only notify the campus about the situation but also will let  people know what kind of action they need to take. <br />
  <br />
  The decision to activate the Eagle Alert system for a message will be made by:  the president, chief of police, provost and vice president of academic affairs,  or vice president for finance and administration.<strong></strong></p>
<p>In order for  the system to work effectively, UNT's students, faculty and staff must add  their phone numbers into the system by logging in at <a href="https://my.unt.edu/psp/papd01/EMPLOYEE/EMPL/?cmd=logout">my.unt.edu</a>. Click on the green <strong>Eagle Alert</strong> banner to go  to a page where you can provide or update three phone numbers – cell, home and  office phone numbers.</p>
<ul>
  <li>On the <strong>Phone Numbers</strong> page, you  also will be able to indicate if you would like text messages sent to your cell phone.</li>
</ul>
<p>Eagle Alert is operated by Connect - ED, a service that NTI Group provides to  universities and schools. </p>
</blockquote>

<p class="montserrat">Eagle Alert notifies campus with immediate emergency information; @UNTEagleAlert on Twitter provides real-time updates</p>
        

<p><strong>Students, staff and faculty should update their phone numbers in <a href="https://my.unt.edu">my.unt.edu</a>.</strong></p>
<p>UNT uses a system called <strong>Eagle Alert</strong> to quickly notify you with critical information in an emergency.</p>
<p>Eagle Alert allows UNT administrators to quickly contact campus community members by phone about <strong>emergency situations</strong> affecting the safety and well being of people on and around the UNT campus. The system <strong>sends voice messages</strong> to the <strong>phones</strong>, including <strong>cell phones</strong>, of all active faculty, staff and students. The system also can send <strong>text messages</strong> to cell phones if you grant the system permission to do so before hand.</p>

        
        <!--blockquote>
          <p>&quot;Whenever  an incident occurs that affects the immediate safety and well being of  the UNT community, the appropriate messages are sent by the system,  informing the community of actions to take to insure their continued  safety,&quot; says <strong>Joe Adamo</strong>, director of Communications Services.</p>
        </blockquote-->
        
        <p>And <a href="https://twitter.com/UNTEagleAlert"><strong>@UNTEagleAlert</strong></a> is UNT's official campus emergency Twitter account. By following @UNTEagleAlert, you will receive emergency messages and updates in real time to keep you informed.</p>
        
        <h3 id="item-1" class="toggler">What's  an emergency?</h3>
        <div id="collapse-item-1" class="toggler-content mo-hide">
            <p>Eagle Alert and @UNTEagleAlert are only used in <strong>critical situations</strong> including:</p>
                <ul>
                    <li>severe weather with imminent dangerous conditions affecting campus, such as tornado warnings</li>
                    <li>campus closings due to inclement weather</li>
                    <li>health and public safety emergencies like chemical spills, fires or violence</li>
                </ul>
            <p>Messages not only notify you about the situation but also let you know what kind of action you need to take.</p>
            
            <p class="vert-spacer">&nbsp;</p>
        </div>
        
        <h3 id="item-2" class="toggler">Who  decides what you will hear?</h3>
        <div id="collapse-item-2" class="toggler-content mo-hide">
          <p>The decision to activate the Eagle Alert system is made by any of the following people:</p>
            <ul>
                <li>President or designee</li>
                <li>Provost</li>
                <li>Vice President for Finance and Administration</li>
                <li>Vice President for Student Affairs</li>
                <li>Chief of Police or designee</li>
                <li>Senior Police Supervisor on duty </li>
                <li>Sr. Risk Management Director or designee</li>
            </ul>
            
            <p class="vert-spacer">&nbsp;</p>
        </div>
        
        <h3 id="item-3" class="toggler">What will happen when the university closes?</h3>
        <div id="collapse-item-3" class="toggler-content mo-hide">
          <p>When a decision is made to close the university, UNT will notify you by:</p>
                <ul>
                    <li>sending an Eagle Alert message</li>
                    <li>providing the information to campus telephone operators</li>
                    <li>posting a message to the university web site</li>
                    <li>posting a message on the <a href="https://www.facebook.com/northtexas">UNT Facebook page</a></li>
                    <li>tweeting a message from <a href="https://twitter.com/UNTEagleAlert">@UNTEagleAlert</a></li>
                    <li>sending campus emails</li>
                    <li>informing news media</li>
                </ul>
            <p>If the university is <strong>closed for more than one day</strong>, you should check the UNT web site at <a href="http://www.unt.edu">www.unt.edu</a>, the UNT Facebook page and <a href="https://twitter.com/UNTEagleAlert">@UNTEagleAlert</a> for the latest information.&nbsp; The university also will continue providing updated information on Facebook and Twitter, to campus telephone operators, and will inform news media.</p>
            
            <p class="vert-spacer">&nbsp;</p>
        </div>
        
        <h3 id="item-4" class="toggler">How  can you make sure it works?</h3>
        <div id="collapse-item-4" class="toggler-content mo-hide">
            <p>All students, faculty and staff are automatically enrolled in the EagleAlert system using the telephone numbers you provided to UNT during your registration or hiring process.&nbsp; You should check your <strong>contact information</strong> regularly and <strong>update it as soon as it changes</strong> by logging in at <a href="https://my.unt.edu">my.unt.edu</a>.</p>
                <ul>
                    <li>on your <strong>My Page</strong>, click on the <strong>Update Eagle Alert</strong> link to go directly to a page where you can update three phone numbers – cell, home and office.&nbsp; In case of an emergency, a message will be sent to all your phone numbers.</li>
                    <li>on the <strong>Phone Numbers page</strong>, you can indicate if you would like text messages sent to your cell phone.</li>
                </ul>
            <p>A message will be sent to <strong>all the numbers you provide </strong>anytime Eagle Alert is used.<br>
            To be sure you'll receive Twitter messages, simply follow <a href="https://twitter.com/UNTEagleAlert">@UNTEagleAlert</a>. Consider turning on your mobile device's notification settings so that you will see our emergency tweets as they are delivered.</p>
            <p>Like other emergency alert systems, Eagle Alert and @UNTEagleAlert are <strong>tested once each fall and spring semester</strong> by University Relations, Communications and Marketing;  Risk Management Services or the UNT Police Department by sending test messages to the university community. Additional limited tests are conducted if needed.</p>
                  
            <p class="vert-spacer">&nbsp;</p>
        </div>
        
        <h3 id="item-5" class="toggler">Who runs the service?</h3>
        <div id="collapse-item-5" class="toggler-content mo-hide">
            <p>Eagle Alert is operated by <a href="http://www.blackboard.com/platforms/connect/overview.aspx">Blackboard Connect</a>, that provides the same product to other universities and schools including Texas Tech, Baylor, University of Dallas and the Richardson Independent School District. UNT's University Relations, Communications and Marketing division manages @UNTEagleAlert.</p>
            
            <p class="vert-spacer">&nbsp;</p>
        </div>
