  <div class="content">
    <p><strong><strong>The Staff Senate is the vehicle for peer leadership among the university staff. The Senate is comprised of elected members who represent more than 2300 staff employees. The Senate receives input from and provides feedback to its constituents.&nbsp;</strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>Mission of the Staff Senate:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><em>to continue to foster communication within the campus community;</em></li>
<li><em>to refer issues and inform the University administration on items that are of interest to the staff;</em></li>
<li><em>to advance the University's mission</em></li>
<li><em>to improve the delivery of services to the University's constituencies</em></li>
<p></p>
<p><strong>Staff Survey Results:</strong></p>
<ul>
<p>Thank you for participating in the Staff Senate survey.  The outcomes of the survey, in the aggregate, have been shared with President Smatresk and members of the President's Cabinet.  Click <a href="https://staffsenate.unt.edu/httpstaffsenateuntedu" title="https://staffsenate.unt.edu/httpstaffsenateuntedu">https://staffsenate.unt.edu/httpstaffsenateuntedu</a>   to view the survey results.</p>
<p>As part of this survey, you were asked to offer questions for submission to President Smatresk.  The questions have been consolidated around central themes &amp; provided to the President in advance of the upcoming Staff Senate meeting.  If you are unable to attend the meeting, his responses to the questions will be posted to the Senate website.</p>
<p>President Smatresk is attending the upcoming Staff Senate meeting, which we hope you attend.</p>
<p><strong>Staff Senate Meeting:</strong></p>
<ul>
<p>Staff Senate Meeting<br />
February 9, 2016 from 3-5 PM<br />
Union Senate Chambers, Suite #332</p>
<p>Thank you, again, for your participation in the survey.  If you have any related questions, feel free to contact the staff senate at <a href="mailto:StaffSenate@unt.edu">StaffSenate@unt.edu</a></p>
</ul>
