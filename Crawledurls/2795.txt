    <h3>Overview</h3>
<p>
It is critical the University of North Texas makes informed decisions about the selection of (and investment in) “common good” information technology services.  Generally, a “common good” service benefits a significant number of users across multiple organizational entities.   IT governance defines both who makes these decisions as well as the process for making those decisions.  UIT embraces a governance model to clearly define and communicate IT requirements, decision responsibilities, and accountabilities to encourage effective deployment of “common good” information technology at UNT. </p>
<h3>Governance Groups</h3>
<h4>Academic and Research IT Group</h4>
<ul>
<li>Purpose (in brief):
<ul>
<li>Align academic and research IT services and resources with university strategic plans and tactical directions</li>
<li>Engage campus constituencies in evaluation, planning, and execution of academic and research IT initiatives</li>
<li>Provide transparency around academic and research IT governance and service provision</li>
<li>Provide a forum for proposal of academic and research IT initiatives and services to provide new or enhanced capabilities</li>
</ul>
</li><li>Membership:
<ul>
<li>Chair: Senior Director of University Information Technology</li>

<li>College of Music </li>

<li>College of Visual Arts and Design </li>
<li>Toulouse Graduate School </li>
<li>Honors College </li>
<li>Mayborn School of Journalism </li>
<li>Texas Academy of Mathematics and Science</li>
</ul>
</li><li>UNT Libraries</li>

<li>Student Computer Lab Governance (non-voting)</li>
<li>Faculty Senate (non-voting)</li>

<li>Director of Space Management and Planning/Classroom Optimization Group (non-voting)</li>
</ul>
<h4>Technical Architecture Group</h4>
<ul>
<li>Purpose (in brief):
<ul>
<li>Receive input from technical staff</li>
<li>Accept, analyze, and evaluate IT-related solutions for shared needs</li>
<li>Provides strong input to UNT Leadership to ensure IT meets the university’s operational needs</li>
<li>Define and coordinate the procedures, standards, and technology requirements UNT uses to organize data, equipment, and applications</li>
</ul>
