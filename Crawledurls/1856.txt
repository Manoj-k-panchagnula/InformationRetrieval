<h2>Barbara Jordan Media Awards</h2>
<!--<p><iframe frameborder="0" height="365" scrolling="auto" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/NVFmdsRU4mQ?wmode=opaque&amp;rel=0&amp;fs=1&amp;showinfo=0&amp;autohide=1" width="650"></iframe><br />
<span style="color: rgb(147, 147, 147); padding-top: 5px; font-family: Verdana,san-serif; font-size: 10px;">Video of Texas Governor Rick Perry congratulating the 2014 Barbara Jordan Media Awardees.</span></p>-->

<p>Each year the Governor&rsquo;s Committee on People with Disabilities, in conjunction with local committees throughout the state, accepts nominations for media professionals and students who have produced material for the public which <strong>accurately and positively reports on individuals with disabilities, using People First language and respectful depictions.</strong> Nominations can be submitted by anyone, including the producer of the piece, by completing the submission form and sending it with any necessary supporting materials to the Governor&rsquo;s Committee on People with Disabilities.</p>

<p><img alt="Gold award medallion with image of Barbara Jordan in profile" src="/files/disabilities/Medallion_FB_Color.JPG" style="float: right;" title="Gold award medallion with image of Barbara Jordan in profile " width="200" /></p>

<p>A panel of judges, including media professionals and people with disabilities, score the entries and collectively select the winners. Each year the decisions for the judges become more difficult as more quality entries are received in the categories of Print Feature, Print News, Photojournalism, TV Feature, TV Documentary, Special Contribution by an Organization, Internet, etc. See <a href="/files/disabilities/BJMA_Submission_Guidelines.pdf"><strong>Submission Guidelines</strong></a> and <a href="/files/disabilities/BJMA_Fillable_Entry_Form.docx"><strong>Entry Form</strong></a> for complete list of categories.</p>

<p>For teachers and students of journalism, <a href="http://gov.texas.gov/disabilities/awards/scoop"><strong>THE SCOOP</strong></a> is an educational video produced by the Texas Governor&#39;s Committee on People with Disabilities. This informative and interactive 45 minute training session titled &quot;The Scoop on Reporting About People with Disabilities&quot; illustrates how to interact with and report about people with disabilities. The video incorporates open captioning and audio description to show media accessibility. The video, lesson plans, and related documents can be viewed for free on our <a href="http://gov.texas.gov/disabilities/awards/scoop"><strong>Website</strong></a>, or teachers can contact the Governor&#39;s Committee on People with Disabilities directly for a DVD and paper copies.</p>

<p>The Barbara Jordan Media Awards are named in honor of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barbara_Jordan"><strong>Houston native and U.S. congresswoman</strong></a> who gained recognition and a place of honor in history. She was known as a remarkable orator who broke barriers and built bridges. She received the Presidential Medal of Freedom and served for one day in 1972 as the Governor of Texas (both Governor Preston Smith and Lt. Governor Ben Barnes were out of state). In 1973, Jordan was diagnosed with multiple sclerosis, and eventually began to use a cane and then a wheelchair. Barbara Jordan passed away in 1996.</p>

<p>&nbsp;The Barbara Jordan Media Award itself is a beautiful three-inch bronze medallion designed by David Deming, an internationally prominent sculptor, and is presented to winners in several categories each year at an awards ceremony hosted by one of the many local committees throughout the state.</p>

<p align="left">&nbsp;Elements in past winning entries include:</p>

<ul>
	<li>Portraying people with disabilities as independent and productive.</li>
	<li>Focusing on stories about people first and disability second.</li>
	<li>Using people with disabilities as sources, rather than as examples.</li>
	<li>Placing coverage in appropriate subject area (e.g. sports covered in sports section).</li>
</ul>

<p align="left">To be eligible, entries must have been produced and offered to the public between <strong>January 1</strong> and <strong>December 31</strong> of the current year and submitted to our office by <strong>January 15</strong> of the following year.</p>
