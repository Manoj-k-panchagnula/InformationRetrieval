    <p><strong><span style="font-size: small;">Browsers</span></strong></p>
<p><a href="https://help.blackboard.com/en-us/Learn/9.1_SP_12_and_SP_13/Student/040_Browser_Support_for_SP_13">Supported Browsers and Operating Systems for Blackboard Learn</a></p>
<p>Blackboard’s support of Google Chrome and Mozilla Firefox differs from the other supported&nbsp;browsers. Chrome and Firefox are following a rapid release strategy and releasing updates&nbsp;every 6 or so weeks. Chrome requires an automatic upgrade for end users, whereas Firefox&nbsp;offers the option to turn off the automatic upgrade. Blackboard has decided to support Chrome&nbsp;and Firefox in the broad sense meaning we will accept issues on both browsers and strive to&nbsp;resolve issues on Blackboard Learn as appropriate.</p>
<p>Blackboard has conducted testing using the Stable Channel of Chrome (Chrome 19) and the&nbsp;Final Release Channel of Firefox (Firefox 12) during testing of this release. We resolved&nbsp;browser related issues at the time of releases, but cannot guarantee the compatibility of future&nbsp;versions. We will conduct testing on the latest browsers and Blackboard Learn releases to&nbsp;provide timely information on browser compatibility.</p>
<p><strong style="font-size: small;">Install Plug-Ins for Blackboard</strong></p>
<p>Some Blackboard courses use browser plug-ins to run certain types of multimedia (e.g. streaming video). To view portions of these courses, you may need one or more of the plug-ins. You may download all of the following, or just come back to this page when you need them.&nbsp;(<em>The following are external links that will take you outside of UNT servers</em>):</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.real.com/" target="_blank">Real Player</a></strong>:<strong>&nbsp;</strong>(linked outside of UNT)<strong>&nbsp;</strong>For viewing streaming video or listening to streaming audio clips</p>
</li>
<li><strong><a href="http://sdc.shockwave.com/shockwave/download/alternates/#fp" target="_blank">Flash</a>:&nbsp;</strong>(linked outside of UNT)<strong>&nbsp;</strong>For viewing animations or using interactive content</li>
<li><strong><a href="http://java.com/en/">Java:</a>&nbsp;</strong>(linked outside of UNT)<strong>&nbsp;</strong>For uploading files and web conferencing</li>
<li>
<p><strong><a href="http://sdc.shockwave.com/shockwave/download/alternates/#sp" target="_blank">Shockwave</a>:&nbsp;</strong>(linked outside of UNT)<strong>&nbsp;</strong>For viewing animations or using interactive content</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.adobe.com/products/acrobat/readstep.html" target="_blank">Acrobat Reader</a></strong>: (linked outside of UNT) For viewing, downloading and printing Adobe PDF files</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.microsoft.com/windows/windowsmedia/players.aspx" target="_blank">Windows Media Player</a></strong>: (linked outside of UNT) For viewing streaming video or listening to streaming audio clips</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong><span style="color: #ff0000;">*</span>Quicktime is no longer supported by Apple when installed on the Windows operating system.&nbsp;</strong>It is reccomended that instructors that wish to use .mov video files in their course contact their <a href="http://clear.unt.edu/consulting">Instructional Consultant</a> to have to video file coverted to a more widely support format.</p>
<div id="_mcePaste" style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">Technologies Not Supported</div>
<div id="_mcePaste" style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">The following technologies are not supported:</div>
<div id="_mcePaste" style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">• Internet Explorer 6, 7</div>
<div id="_mcePaste" style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">• Firefox 1.x, 2.0, 3.0, and 3.5</div>
<div id="_mcePaste" style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">• Safari 2.0, 3.x and any version on Windows</div>
<div id="_mcePaste" style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">• Mac OSX 10.3, 10.4 “Tiger”</div>
<div id="_mcePaste" style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">• Java 5, although it may continue to work</div>
<p><strong><span style="font-size: small;">Turning Off Popup Blockers</span></strong></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 17.328125px;"><span style="font-size: small;">If you are unable to view discussion postings, quiz windows, and download links, then you may need to disable pop-up blocking.<br />How to disable popups for: <a href="http://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/pop-blocker-settings-exceptions-troubleshooting">Firefox</a>, <a href="http://support.google.com/chrome/bin/answer.py?hl=en&amp;answer=95472">Chrome</a>, <a href="http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/Windows7/Internet-Explorer-Pop-up-Blocker-frequently-asked-questions#">Internet Explorer</a></span></span></p>
<p><strong><span style="font-size: small;">Technologies Not Supported</span></strong></p>
<p style="margin: 0.7em 0px; padding: 0px; color: #333333; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12px; line-height: 14.390625px;">The following technologies are&nbsp;not&nbsp;supported in SP 13:</p>
<ul style="margin: 0.6em 0px 1em; padding: 0px 0px 0px 1.25em; list-style-position: initial; list-style-image: initial; color: #333333; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12px; line-height: 14.390625px;">
<li style="margin: 0.6em 0px 0px 0.4em; padding: 0px;"><span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;" data-mce-mark="1">Internet Explorer 6, 7, 11</span></li>
<li style="margin: 0.6em 0px 0px 0.4em; padding: 0px;"><span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;" data-mce-mark="1">Firefox 1.x, 2.0, 3.0, 3.5, and 3.6</span></li>
<li style="margin: 0.6em 0px 0px 0.4em; padding: 0px;"><span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;" data-mce-mark="1">Safari 2.0, 3.x and any version on Windows</span></li>
<li style="margin: 0.6em 0px 0px 0.4em; padding: 0px;"><span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;" data-mce-mark="1">Windows XP 64-bit</span></li>
<li style="margin: 0.6em 0px 0px 0.4em; padding: 0px;"><span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;" data-mce-mark="1">Mac OSX 10.3, 10.4, 10.5</span></li>
<li style="margin: 0.6em 0px 0px 0.4em; padding: 0px;"><span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;" data-mce-mark="1">Java 5, although it may continue to work</span></li>
</ul>
<p><strong><span style="font-size: small;">Deprecation of Supported Technologies</span></strong></p>
<p>Deprecation is a standard software practice to notify the community of future removal of&nbsp;support. These technologies are still tested and expected to perform in the intervening releases&nbsp;until the release in which they are removed from support.</p>
<p><span style="white-space: pre;"> </span>• Mac OS X 10.5 “Leopard” will no longer be supported with the release of 9.1 Service&nbsp;Pack 10.</p>  </div>
