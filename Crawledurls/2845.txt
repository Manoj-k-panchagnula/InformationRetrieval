<div class="content-list-component mt-paragraph text"><p>Friendly neighbors, family-run businesses, and quaint restaurants serving comforting diner fare--these are the images conjured up by the phrase "Main Street USA." Even in this fast-paced era, many small towns have held onto their historic roots and preserved their central thoroughfares. From a harbor town on the west coast with ornate Victorian architecture, to Key West's colorful Duval Street, visitors can find unique cultural attractions and warm, welcoming communities in small towns across the country. A few steps along each of these main streets prove that small-town America is alive and well--and can hold its own against larger, neighboring cities. Here are our picks for America's best main streets.--<em>Emily Wasserman</em></p></div>

<div class="listicle__slide-caption js-image-caption">Galena’s Main Street epitomizes small town America. Just three hours outside Chicago, <a href="http://www.fodors.com/world/north-america/usa/illinois/side-trips-from-chicago/galena-illinois/" target="_blank">Galena</a> provides a retreat from urban hustle and bustle. The downtown area includes antique shops, restaurants, and boutiques—all housed in mid-19th century storefronts. Visitors can browse the work of local artists at the street’s art galleries or stop by the DeSoto House Hotel for a breakfast or lunch buffet. Those with a sweet tooth will enjoy Galena’s old-fashioned ice cream parlor. Guests can choose from more than 40 flavors of ice cream and sample old-fashioned sodas, floats, malts, and shakes.

<div class="listicle__slide-caption js-image-caption">Once named the “City of Dreams,” <a href="http://www.fodors.com/world/north-america/usa/washington/the-olympic-peninsula-and-washington-coast/port-townsend/" target="_blank">Port Townsend</a> was slated to become the West Coast’s largest harbor. Plans changed—but the town still retains much of its original charm. Visit Water Street to explore historic business districts and watch Victorian ships sail across the water. Port Townsend Gallery showcases the work of some of the town’s best artists, and visitors can sample house-made ales at Port Townsend Brewing Co. Stop by The Spice &amp; Tea Exchange for gourmet seasonings and spices as well as custom-made rubs and blends. 

<em>Plan Your Trip</em>: Visit Fodor’s <a href="http://www.fodors.com/world/north-america/usa/washington/the-olympic-peninsula-and-washington-coast/port-townsend/" target="_blank">Port Townsend Guide</a>

<div class="listicle__slide-caption js-image-caption">Just north of Dallas, Denton boasts a vibrant community with attractions for every age. Main Street is located next to the city’s historic courthouse and features unique shops, numerous dining options, and a variety of museums, galleries, and art venues. Watch live performances outside the courthouse, or browse one of the street’s antique stores. A Creative Art Studio features the work of local artist and offers classes for budding artists. For a sweet treat, visit Denton Square Donuts: the store is open during evening hours so you can satisfy your late night cravings. 

<em>Plan Your Trip</em>: Visit Fodor's <a href="http://www.fodors.com/world/north-america/usa/texas/" target="_blank">Texas Guide</a>

<div class="listicle__slide-caption js-image-caption">This picturesque Vermont town belongs on the back of a postcard. Tree-lined streets, pristine homes, and quaint shops make <a href="http://www.fodors.com/world/north-america/usa/vermont/central-vermont/woodstock/" target="_blank">Woodstock</a> an ideal travel destination. The town’s Center Street features rustic brick buildings and ends at a park overlooking a picturesque river bridge. Get your hands dirty at <a href="http://www.fodors.com/world/north-america/usa/vermont/central-vermont/review-95940.html" target="_blank">Billings Farm &amp; Museum</a>. Guests can learn how to milk a cow or participate in a butter-making workshop.  Stop by the artist owned and operated Collective- The Art of Craft, to browse the work of local artists. End the day at The Lincoln Inn’s Mangowood restaurant, which offers fresh, seasonal fare with a creative twist. 

Plan Your Trip: Visit <a href="http://www.fodors.com/world/north-america/usa/vermont/central-vermont/woodstock/" target="_blank">Fodor’s Woodstock Guide</a>

The city oozes Southern charm and its famous Historic District features sprawling mansions, horse-drawn carriages, and palm tree-lined paths. The street is divided into North and South regions, each with their own distinct character. Visit <a href="http://www.fodors.com/world/north-america/usa/south-carolina/charleston/north-of-broad/" target="_blank">North of Broad</a> for shopping and delicious eateries, or travel south for oak-shaded parks and picturesque private homes. Take a tour of the <a href="http://www.fodors.com/world/north-america/usa/south-carolina/charleston/review-104768.html" target="_blank">Nathaniel Russell House</a> for an up-close look at ornate, 19th-century architecture. <a href="http://www.fodors.com/world/north-america/usa/south-carolina/charleston/review-577960.html" target="_blank">City Market</a>, on the street’s north side, features open-air stalls with handmade crafts, produce, and regional souvenirs.

<div class="listicle__slide-caption js-image-caption">Also known as the “Queen of Spas,” <a href="http://www.fodors.com/world/north-america/usa/new-york/albany-and-central-new-york/saratoga-springs/" target="_blank">Saratoga Springs</a> was named for its abundant mineral waters. Now a popular horseracing destination, the town features a historic main street with colonial-style buildings, boutiques, and benches perfect for people watching. Stop by the <a href="http://www.fodors.com/world/north-america/usa/new-york/albany-and-central-new-york/" target="_blank">Saratoga Farmer’s Market</a> for fresh, local produce, poultry, and baked goods, or head to the <a href="http://www.fodors.com/world/north-america/usa/new-york/albany-and-central-new-york/saratoga-springs/activities_h2232544_horse-racing.html" target="_blank">Saratoga Racecourse</a> to watch thoroughbreds compete for the top title. For authentic Saratoga-style barbeque, look no further than<a href="http://www.fodors.com/world/north-america/usa/new-york/albany-and-central-new-york/review-437837.html" target="_blank"> PJ’s</a>; the 50s style drive-in is located on the city’s main street, and visitors can enjoy tender ribs and brisket at outdoor picnic tables.

Browse regional artwork at the Centre Street Arts Gallery, or enjoy music from local artists at the Chocolate Church Arts Center. Visit Front Street to explore quaint antique shops and bookstores, or stop by Centre Street’s natural market for a taste of fresh produce.

Locals and tourists love Local Flavor Café, an eclectic eatery with fresh, creative cuisine. Dance the night away at Eureka Paradise, one of the city’s most popular nightclubs, or enjoy live music and pub fare at Chelsea’s Corner Café. </div>

Adults and children will enjoy Chutters, a sweets shop that boasts the world’s longest candy counter. To experience the town’s industrial history, visit <a href="http://www.fodors.com/world/north-america/usa/new-hampshire/the-white-mountains/" target="_blank">Littleton Grist Mill</a> on nearby Mill Street. The restored 18th-century mill includes a small shop and museum with original equipment.
