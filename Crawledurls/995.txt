			<h2>
				Who We Are</h2>

			<p>
				The Student Government Association is the official voice of the undergraduate student body at the University of North Texas. As an organization, we are committed to making each student&rsquo;s opinions and concerns heard in the administration. The Student Government Association is comprised of an Executive Branch, Student Senate, Supreme Court, and the Freshman Interns. Together, these factions work with students across the University and administration to make our campus better for everyone.</p>
		</div>

		<div class="col-md-6">
			<h2>
				What We Do</h2>

			<p>
				The Student Government Association is a multi-purpose organization that can grant funding to students for conferences or programming, promote other student organization&rsquo;s programs, as well as host University wide events. Please take a moment to explore our website to learn more information about our executive board, senators, and the different ways that SGA can benefit you, the student. Please feel free to contact us with any questions or concerns, or come by our office in Union 344 to meet with us in person.</p>

	<div class="col-md-8">
		<p>
			The University of North Texas Student Government Association, much like our country&#39;s branches of government,&nbsp;is composed of three main branches: the Executive Branch, the Legislative Branch, and the Judicial Branch. Together these three branches work to represent the student body here at the University of North Texas.</p>

		<p>
			<strong>The Executive Branch</strong> is comprised of eight (8) executive board&nbsp;members: Student Body President, Student Body Vice President, Chief of Staff, Director of Student Affairs, Director of Leadership and Development, Director of Campus Outreach, Director of Public Relations and Projects Manager. The executive powers of the Association are vested in this branch.&nbsp;</p>

		<p>
			<a href="https://studentaffairs.unt.edu/freshman-intern-program" id="text_link"><strong>The Freshman Intern Program </strong></a> is&nbsp;the Ultimate Leadership Experience. A selective group of about 30 first year freshman students work together to learn communication skills, get access to limited networking opportunities with university administrators and staff. These students will be trained in essential skills about leadership that will build and prepare them to be the principal influences on campus.<strong> </strong></p>

		<p>
			<a href="http://sga.unt.edu/student-senate" id="text_link"><strong>The Student Senate</strong></a> is a body of forty five (45) made up of representatives from each of the colleges. The seats are proportional to the student population of that particular college. The Student Senate is the legislative body of the Association.</p>

		<p>
			<a class="button button-constitution" href="/sites/default/files/untsgacodeofconduct_1.pdf">Senate Code of Conduct</a></p>

		<p>
			<strong>To speak to one of your representatives come into the SGA office during one of the senator&#39;s office hours listed on their staff profile.</strong></p>

		<p>
			<strong>The Judicial Branch</strong> is made up of our Supreme Court, and the Advocate General. This branch deals with hearing regarding the UNT SGA Governing Documents. Additionally,&nbsp;branch handles code of conduct referrals made by members of UNT SGA against members of the House and Senate.&nbsp;</p>

		<p>
			These branches work hard to represent the diverse student population through important legislation, referendums, and forums. We strive to get students involved and informed in UNT events and organizations.</p>
