<h3>Tickets</h3>
<p>Season tickets for the 2014-15 schedule will go on sale in late summer or early fall, and single-game tickets soon after. Individual-game tickets are $12 for Premium Reserved seats and $7 for Reserved seats. Also available is the Family Pack for $35 for one game, and includes four tickets, four food items, and four drinks from the concessions stands. Group ticket packages start at $6 per ticket for groups of fifteen or more. For more information, contact the North Texas Athletics Ticket Office at 940-565-2527. <a href="http://www.meangreenmap.com/superpitseating.html" target="_blank">Click here for a chart of Super Pit seating</a>.</p>

<h3>The Super Pit</h3>
<p>The North Texas Coliseum, better known as The Super Pit, is home to Mean Green men's and women's basketball and to a long and colorful history.</p>
<p>Located at 600 Avenue D, the nickname Super Pit derives from the Coliseum’s predecessor, the Men’s Gym, which was known as the Snake Pit. In 1973, North Texas basketball moved across Highland Street to the Super Pit, trading 5,000 wood-bleacher seats for 10,500 padded, individual seats. The arena was updated in 2013 with a new court and a center-hung scoreboard. <a class="mainlink" href="http://www.meangreenmap.com/superpit.html" target="_blank">Click here for more information on the The Super Pit</a>.</p>

<h3>Super Pit Parking</h3>
<p>There are two parking options on gameday at the Super Pit: preferred parking for Mean Green Club members with a parking pass and parking for the general public.</p>
<p>Mean Green Club parking is on Highland Street and the northern-most portion of the Fouts Field lot. Mean Green Club members may also parking in the general section of the Fouts Field lot. Access is from North Texas Blvd.</p>
<p>General parking is in the Fouts Field lot, access from North Texas Blvd. Parking is free.</p>
<p><a class="mainlink" href="http://www.meangreenmap.com/superpitparking.html" target="_blank">Click here for a map of Super Pit game-day parking</a>.</p>

<h3>The Tony Benford Show</h3>
<p>MGRN broadcasts <i>The Tony Benford Show</i> on Monday evenings during basketball season, featuring the Mean Green men's head basketball coach. The show is broadcast live from 7:30 to 8 p.m. from Rudy's BBQ in Denton, located at 520 Interstate 35E. The show is preceded by <i>The Mike Petersen Show</i>, featuring the North Texas women's head basketball coach, from 7 to 7:30.</p>

<h3>Mean Green Club</h3>
<p>The Mean Green Club raises funds for North Texas athletic scholarships. Donations to the Mean Green Club help support nearly 350 North Texas student-athletes each year and provide them with the best collegiate experience possible.</p>
<p>Members receive exclusive access to North Texas athletics and year-long benefits such as invitations to member-only events, the best parking and seating available, and more.
