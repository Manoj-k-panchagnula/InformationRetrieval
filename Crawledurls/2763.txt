        <h1 class="pagetitle">Parking Permit Information:</h1>
        <!-- Content unit - One column -->
        <div class="column1-unit">

          <h2>I already pay for tuition, why do I need to pay  for a parking permit?</h2>
          <p>Parking is an auxiliary operation. It receives no direct funding from  either the State of Texas or the University. As such, the program  operates through permits, fines, and fees generated by students,  faculty, staff, and visitors who <u>choose </u>to park on campus.</p>
          <h2>Where does my permit or citation money go?</h2>
            <p>Major uses of parking funds include: parking lot maintenance and  construction; parking and traffic activities related to special events  management; police and security-related operations; including, post  9/11 and Virginia Tech continuing security enhancements, and operation  and enforcement of parking lots in accordance with our parking rules  and regulations.</p>
          <h2>Why do we need to increase parking permit rates?</h2>
            <p>Student parking permit rates were last increased in Fall 2011.  Faculty/staff parking permits were last increased in Fall 2011. Our  costs to provide and operate our parking program have increased  significantly since Fall 2003:</p>
          <ul>
            <li><u><strong>Asphalt for repaving</strong></u> – on the average increased 74+% with significant expected continuing  increases as oil prices continue to rise in the marketplace</li>
            <li><u><strong>Cement for concrete paving</strong></u> – on the  average  increased 160+% with additional  increases expected  in the short term</li>
            <li><u><strong>Labor and Benefit Costs</strong> </u>– increased an  average of 26%</li>
            <li><u><strong>Surface Lot parking spaces</strong> </u>– costs  generally range from $2,000 - $6,000 per space to construct</li>
            <li><u><strong>Parking Garage spaces</strong> </u>– costs generally  range from $12,000 - $20,000 per space to construct</li>
          </ul>
          <p><u><strong>Priorities  and progress towards the UNT Master Plan</strong></u> -  As we move into the future, our UNT Master Plan directs us to  continue developing our academic and institutional facilities in such a  manner that we’ll be required to transition additional surface parking  in favor of parking garages and structures. Because garages and  structures are extremely expensive to build and operate the Master Plan  encourages significant growth and support of other transportation  options; such as shuttle buses, bicycles, carpools, vanpools, the  proposed DCTA rail system, etc. to help us continue to reduce the need  for individually operated motor vehicles on campus. </p>
          <h2>Why are parking permit rates different for  various types of permits?</h2>
            <p>Permit prices are established based upon a number of  factors; primarily including:</p>
          <ul>
            <li><u><strong>Location of spaces</strong></u> – the closer or more preferred the location the more expensive. The  same principle is used at the airport where ‘terminal” parking is  significantly more expensive than “remote” parking.</li>
            <li><u><strong>Privilege  conferred by permit</strong></u> – “A” spaces are individually reserved spaces and carry a premium of  125% over the cost of regular faculty/staff  “D” and premium “P” spaces  which are in preferred locations but only provide a space “as  available.” </li>
            <p> Remember: </p>
            </p>
            <li>It is possible to attend UNT for four (4)  years, park on campus every day and never receive a parking ticket if  you comply with the parking rules and regulations.</li>
            <li>We  always have parking available on class days; however, that parking may  not be next to the building or the individual’s parking lot of choice.</li>
            <li>We  encourage the use of the shuttle system, car pools, bicycling, walking,  etc. to save money, time wasted in traffic, reduce vehicle emissions,  and reduce fuel demand. </li>
          </ul>
