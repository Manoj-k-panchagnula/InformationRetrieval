field-label-inline clearfix"><div class="field-label">Office:&nbsp;</div><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even">ENV 310Q</div></div></div><div class="field href="https://faculty.unt.edu/editprofile.php?onlyview=1&amp;pid=1330">https://faculty.unt.edu/editprofile.php?onlyview=1&amp;pid=1330</a></div></div></div><div class="field field-name-field-faculty-highlights field-type-text field-label-above"><div class="field-label">Highlights:&nbsp;</div><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even">Dr. Oppong is a medical geographer with a geographic focus on Africa and North America.</div><div class="field-item odd">His research interests include neighborhood characteristics and HIV/AIDS in Texas, geographic distribution of tuberculosis genotypes, and applications of GIS to understanding spatial patterns of disease and health.</div></div></div><div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><p>Ongoing research projects include climate and Buruli Ulcer in Africa, schistosomiasis in Ghana, urban slum health, and computational epidemiology - employing computer simulations to understand geographic patterns of disease spread.  Dr. Oppong teaches a variety of courses including <em>Quantitative Methods in Geography</em>, <em>Medical Geography, and </em>Contemporary<em> Sub-Saharan Africa</em>.   Dr. Oppong advises graduate student research in areas that correspond with his own research interests.  Recent theses include a GIS for UNT campus emergency disaster response, modeling Buruli Ulcer prevalence in Ghana, geographic patterns of tuberculosis among Hispanics, and Lyme disease in Texas.</p>
