			<h2 class="title" id="page-title">How do I apply for scholarships &amp; financial aid?</h2>
			
			        
                <div class="block block-system block-main block-system-main odd block-without-title" id="block-system-main">
  <div class="block-inner clearfix">
                
    <div class="content clearfix">
      <div  about="/freshman/how-to-apply-for-scholarships-and-financial-aid" typeof="sioc:Item foaf:Document" id="node-page-182" class="ds-1col node node-page view-mode-full node-not-promoted node-not-sticky author-wdc odd clearfix clearfix">

  
  <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded"><h3 class="sub-title">Let us guide you through the process</h3>

<p>UNT annually awards $330 million in financial aid, including more than $39 million in scholarships. About 75 percent of UNT students receive financial aid and scholarships.</p>

<hr />

<h3>Award Eligibility and Credit Hours</h3>

<p>Each scholarship or financial aid award requires you to be enrolled in a minimum number of credit hours to be eligible to receive the award. You can view the minimum enrollment requirements for each of your awards at <a href="https://my.unt.edu"  target="_blank" title="MyUNT (opens in a new window)">my.unt.edu</a>.</p>

<p>The difference between full-time, 3/4 time and 1/2 time enrollment status for undergraduate students is:</p>

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr>
      <th>Enrollment Status</th>
      <th>Fall or Spring Semesters</th>
      <th>Summer</th>
    </tr>
</thead>

<tbody>
    <tr>
      <td>Full-time</td>
      <td>12 or more credit hours</td>
      <td>12 or more credit hours</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>3/4 time</td>
      <td>9-11 credit hours</td>
      <td>9-11 credit hours</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>1/2 time</td>
      <td>6-8 credit hours</td>
      <td>6-8 credit hours</td>
    </tr>
  </tbody>
</table>

<hr />

<h3>Scholarships</h3>

<h4>What is a scholarship?</h4>

<p>A scholarship is a financial award given to a student on the basis of academic achievement and promise. However, some also take into account financial need. Scholarships do not have to be repaid. There are 3 types of scholarships you can apply for: university scholarships, department-sponsored scholarships and external (non-UNT) scholarships.</p>

<p><a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/apps/index.php/untexcellence" title="UNT merit scholarship calculator (opens in a new window)" target="_blank">Calculate your UNT merit scholarship award.</a></p>

<p><a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/scholarships" target="_blank" title="UNT scholarships information (opens in a new window)">Learn more about scholarships.</a></p>

<h4>How to apply for scholarships</h4>

<ol>

  <li><h5>The first step in the scholarship process is to <a href="http://www.applytexas.org/" target="_blank" title="Apply for admission to UNT online at ApplyTexas.org (opens in a new window)">apply for admission to UNT</a>.</h5> <br />Scholarship dollars are limited and are awarded on merit and on a first-come, first-served basis. If you complete your admission application early, you can be considered for admission early, and place yourself in the best position to be considered for available funds.</li>

  <li><h5>The next step in the scholarship process is to complete the <a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/scholarships/apply-scholarships" target="_blank" title="How to apply for UNT scholarships (opens in a new window)">General Scholarship Application</a> by March 1.</h5> <br />You should submit this application annually to be considered for any scholarship opportunities available during the upcoming academic year. You do not have to already be accepted to UNT to complete the General Scholarship Application. All you need to apply for the General Scholarship is an active EUID, which you will receive instructions for how to activate once you have applied for admission to UNT. However, in order to be selected for any UNT scholarship award, you must be admitted to a UNT degree seeking program and meet the minimum eligibility requirements of the scholarship by the scholarship deadline.</li>

  <li><h5>Apply to any department-sponsored scholarships.</h5> <br />Each department has their own application process, which will detail the criteria for scholarship consideration. Typically, departmental scholarship awards are made to students with specific program involvement with the department. Students should contact departments from their major or minor area of study to find out what scholarships are offered and about the scholarship application process.</li>

</ol>

<p>We encourage you to complete the <a href="http://fafsa.gov/" target="_blank" title="Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) (opens in a new window)">Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA)</a> annually since some scholarships require the application. There is no cost to apply and no commitment to accept any award eligibility identified.</p>

<p><a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/scholarships/apply-scholarships" target="_blank" title="Apply for scholarships at UNT (opens in a new window)">Apply for scholarships.</a></p>

<h4>When will you know if you receive a scholarship award?</h4>

<h5>UNT Scholarships</h5>

<p>Students selected for a scholarship based on the completion of the <a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/scholarships/apply-scholarships" target="_blank" title="UNT scholarships information (opens in a new window)">General Scholarship Application</a> are notified through <a href="http://eagleconnect.unt.edu/" target="_blank" title="EagleConnect, UNT's official email service (opens in a new window)">EagleConnect</a>, UNT's official email service. Monitor EagleConnect and <a href="https://my.unt.edu" target="_blank" title="MyUNT student account portal (opens in a new window)">my.unt.edu</a> for award information. Most awards will be announced by late June for the Fall semester.</p>

<h5>Departmental Scholarships</h5>

<p>Each department on campus makes departmental scholarship award decisions. Award information is coordinated with SFAS to post the awards to the student's account.</p>

<h4>How do you accept a scholarship award and when will you get your money?</h4>

<p><strong>Once you have received a scholarship, you must accept it to get the money.</strong> Scholarship funds will not disburse to your student account until your awards have been accepted online or your external scholarship funds have been received by UNT.</p>

<p><a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/check-your-award-status-0" target="_blank" title="How to check your financial award status (opens in a new window)">Learn how to check your scholarship award status and accept your awards.</a></p>

<hr />
<h3>Financial Aid</h3>
<h4>What is financial aid?</h4>
<p>The University of North Texas offers a variety of options to assist you in paying for your college education. Costs should not be a barrier to opportunity. Learn about different ways to finance your education, which include <a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/scholarships" target="_blank" title="UNT scholarship information (opens in a new window)">scholarships</a>, <a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/grants" target="_blank" title="UNT grant information (opens in a new window)>grants</a>, <a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/work-study" target="_blank" title="UNT work-study information (opens in a new window)">work-study</a>, and <a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/loans" target="_blank" title="UNT loan information (opens in a new window)">loans</a>.</p>
<p>You might be surprised to find out how much financial aid  you can receive, regardless of your family&rsquo;s income level. You won&rsquo;t know what you are eligible for unless you apply!</p>
<p><a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/overview-financial-aid-process" target="_blank" title="UNT's financial aid process (opens in a new window)">Get an overview of financial aid options and eligibility requirements.</a></p>

<h4>What does financial aid pay for?</h4>

<p>Prior to receiving your refund, Financial Aid will pay all tuition, fees and housing charges, if you are living on campus.</p>

<p><strong>Housing Deposit</strong> - Financial aid funds will not disburse until shortly before the beginning of classes; therefore, you need to plan on paying the pre-payment that Housing and Residence Life requires. For more information regarding the pre-payment, contact Housing at <a href="mailto:housinginfo@unt.edu">housinginfo@unt.edu</a> or 940-565-2610.</p>

<p><strong>Books</strong> - Financial aid funds will not disburse until shortly before the beginning of classes; therefore, you need to plan to pay for your books out of pocket at orientation if you want to purchase them early. The UNT Bookstore does offer a textbook reservation service. To learn more about this service, contact the UNT Bookstore at 940-565-2592 or stop by the University Union while on campus.</p>

<p><strong>Orientation Fees</strong> - Financial aid does not pay for orientation fees. However, you may qualify for orientation fee deferment or fee waivers. Contact  <a href="https://studentaffairs.unt.edu/orientation" target="_blank" title="UNT Orientation website (opens in a new window)">Orientation and Transition Programs</a> for more information.</p>

<h4>How to apply for financial aid</h4>
<ol>

<li><h5>Submit your FAFSA as early as possible.</h5> <br />

The <a href="http://fafsa.gov/" target="_blank" title="Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) website (opens in a new window)"
>Free Application for Federal Student Aid, or FAFSA</a>, is an application that helps colleges and universities determine your eligibility for financial aid. In fact, nearly all students who complete the FAFSA receive some type of financial assistance. <br /><br />

Be sure to submit your FAFSA for the correct school year. The FAFSA is available beginning January of each year for the following fall/spring/summer. UNT's school code is 003594. <a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/step-1" target="_blank" title="More information about submitting the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) (opens in a new window)"> Learn more about submitting the FAFSA.</a> <br /><br />

To receive first consideration for awards, we recommend you complete the FAFSA as early as possible before the <a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/priority-dates-and-deadlines" target="_blank" title="UNT financial aid priority deadlines (opens in a new window)"
>priority deadlines</a>. If you miss the priority deadlines, you can still submit your application, but your financial aid will be delayed and you will need to make appropriate payment arrangements through <a href="https://studentaccounting.unt.edu" target="_blank" title="UNT Student Accounting website (opens in a new window)"
>Student Accounting</a> to ensure your classes are not dropped.</li>

<li><h5>Apply for admission to UNT.</h5> <br />

You must be admitted to UNT before we can process your financial aid. <a href="/freshman/how-to-apply">Learn how to apply for admission to UNT.</a></li>

</ol>
<p><strong><a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/finaidsteps" target="_blank" title="UNT financial aid guide (opens in a new window)"
>View our step-by-step guide to applying for and receiving financial aid.</a></strong></p>

<h4>When will you know if you are receiving financial aid and when will you get your money?</h4>

<p>Student Financial Aid and Scholarships will receive your FAFSA data electronically shortly after you complete your FAFSA and you will receive a Student Aid Report (SAR). <a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/applying-financial-aid-step-5" title="Receiving and reviewing your Student Aid Report (opens in a new window)" target="_blank">Learn more about receiving and reviewing your SAR.</a></p>

<p>It usually takes about six weeks to process your financial aid award package once UNT receives your FAFSA data from the Federal Processor. Once that happens, you'll receive additional instructions on how to complete your financial aid file via your To Do List in your <a href="https://my.unt.edu/" target="_blank" title="MyUNT student account portal (opens in a new window)">my.unt.edu</a> account. Please respond to all requests in a timely manner. Your file must be complete and you must be admitted to UNT before we can process your aid and offer you any financial awards. <a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/access-do-list-myunt" title="How to access your To Do List on your MyUNT student account portal (opens in a new window)" target="_blank">See instructions for accessing your To Do List on my.unt.edu.</a></p>

<p>If selected for a financial award, you will be notified via <a href="https://eagleconnect.unt.edu/" target="_blank" title="EagleConnect, the official UNT email system (opens in a new window)">EagleConnect</a>, UNT's official email service. Monitor EagleConnect and <a href="http://my.unt.edu/" target="_blank" title="MyUNT student account portal (opens in a new window)">my.unt.edu</a> for award information.</p>

<p>For early applicants who are newly admitted to UNT (new students), awards are made in late April/early May for the upcoming year. Most awards will be announced by late June for the Fall semester. Awards will continue to be made throughout the academic year.</p>

<p><a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/how-check-your-financial-aid-status" target="_blank" title="How to check your financial aid award status (opens in a new window)">Check your financial aid award status.</a></p>



<h4>How to accept your financial aid awards</h4>

<p>You can accept your financial aid awards online through <a href="http://my.unt.edu/" target="_blank" title="MyUNT student account portal (opens in a new window)">my.unt.edu</a>. <strong>Financial aid funds will not disburse to your student account until your financial aid awards have been accepted online.</strong></p>

<p><a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/Step-5" target="_blank" title="How to accept your financial aid awards (opens in a new window)">See instructions for accepting your financial aid awards using my.unt.edu.</a></p>

<hr />

<h3>How to check if all of your scholarships and financial aid awards have disbursed</h3>

<p>The Student Financial Aid and Scholarships office processes all financial aid, loans, grants and scholarships.</p>

<p><strong>Financial aid funds will not disburse to your student account until your financial aid awards have been accepted online.</strong></p>

<p>Once the awards are approved and funded (eg: student loans are received from the bank/lender), any anticipated aid is removed from your account and the Student Accounting department posts the amounts to your student account.</p>

<p>View your account summary at <a href="https://my.unt.edu/" target="_blank" title="MyUNT student account portal (opens in a new window)">my.unt.edu</a> to determine if any award has been posted to your account.</p>

<p><a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/step-7" target="_blank" title="How to check your funds disbursement (opens in a new window)">See how to check the disbursal status of your funds.</a></p>

<hr />

<h3>Still have questions about scholarships and financial aid?</h3>

<p>We&rsquo;re here to help you. For more information, call us toll-free at 1-877-881-1014 or drop by the Student Financial Aid and Scholarships office on the first and second floors of the Eagle Student Services Center. <a href="http://maps.unt.edu/places/1017/view" target="_blank" title="UNT Maps (opens in a new window)" >See map</a>. Counselors and Counselor Assistants are available to assist you Monday through Friday from 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., no appointments necessary.</p></div></div></div></div>
