                    <h1 class="page-header">Graduate Program</h1>
                                      <div class="region region-content">
    <section id="block-system-main" class="block block-system clearfix">

      
  <div id="node-24" class="node node-page clearfix">

  
      
  
  <div class="content">
    <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><h2>M.S. in Geography</h2>
<p>The Department of Geography and the Environment offers a Master's of Science (M.S.) degree in geography.  Our program provides students with the ability to apply geographic concepts and techniques to significant societal and environmental challenges.</p>
<p>Beyond a small number of required core courses (GEOG 5160, 5190/5185 and 5800), we do not confine our students to a rigid curriculum. Students work with their major professor to construct a degree plan emphasizing a particular theme or themes and to determine which courses they should take and when.</p>
<p>Students may pursue one of the suggested tracks below, or use one of these tracks as a guide to constructing their degree plan.  Possible tracks of study include:</p>
<ul><li><a href="/node/58" rel="nofollow">Applied Geomorphology</a></li>
<li><a href="/node/61" rel="nofollow">Applied GIS</a></li>
<li><a href="/node/62" rel="nofollow">Archaeology</a></li>
<li><a href="/node/175" rel="nofollow">Business Geography</a></li>
<li><a href="/node/344" rel="nofollow">Globalization, Development and Cities</a></li>
<li><a href="http://geography.unt.edu/http%3A/geography.unt.edu/academic-programs/graduate-program/health-and-medical-geography#overlay-context=http%253A/geography.unt.edu/academic-programs/graduate-program/health-and-medical-geography" rel="nofollow">Health and Medical Geography</a></li>
<li><a href="/node/60" rel="nofollow">Urban Environmental Management</a></li>
<li><a href="/node/59" rel="nofollow">Water Resources Management</a></li>
</ul><p>The department's faculty has expertise in additional areas that may serve as the core focus of a degree plan, including cultural ecology, ecosystem geography, and energy geography. Students may also complete an optional six credit hour minor. Generally, the six hours come from a single department outside of Geography and the Environment.</p>
<h1>Ph.D. in Environmental Science</h1>
<p>The Department of Geography and the Environment works closely with the <a href="http://ias.unt.edu/" rel="nofollow">Institute of Applied Science (IAS)</a>, which coordinates a <a href="http://environmentalscience.unt.edu/Degree%20Programs" rel="nofollow">Ph.D. in Environmental Science</a>. Students interested in pursuing a Ph.D. in geographical aspects of environmental science should consult a geography faculty member with similar research interests. In consultation with the IAS, this faculty member will identify suitable prerequisite coursework and help the student assemble an appropriate degree plan.</p>
