		<h3>
			What does it cost to see a provider or nurse?</h3>

		<p>
			Payment of the Medical Services Fee, which is built into the student&#39;s tuition and fees, allows access to the Student Health and Wellness Center. A student will incur discounted charges for services such as, but not limited to: &nbsp;lab, x-ray, treatments, injections, pharmacy, nutritional consultation, and specialty physician visits.</p>

		<h3>
			Will my insurance pay for my visit?</h3>

		<h3>
			What must I present in order for you to file on the University endorsed student insurance?</h3>

		<p>
			Students must present a current insurance card and the policyholder&#39;s information, including UNT ID number and date of birth.</p>

		<h3>
			How and where do I pay?</h3>

		<p>
			Payment in full is expected at the time of service in the form of cash, check, or credit card. Enrolled students may complete a charge account agreement. All balances must be paid in full prior to the end of the month to avoid a block on the student&#39;s account.</p>

		<h3>
			Pharmacy</h3>

		<p>
			A full-service pharmacy is located inside Chestnut Hall. Prescriptions from private physicians can be filled at the pharmacy, which accepts some insurance.&nbsp;<a href="https://studentaffairs.unt.edu/pharmacy">Click here</a>&nbsp;for more information about insurance policies the pharmacy accepts.</p>

		<h3>
			How does the Health Center compare against other facilities for pricing of services?</h3>

		<p>
			The Student Health and Wellness Center is often less expensive than other facilities for most services based on cash costs of services.&nbsp; Check out our current <a href="/sites/default/files/healthcenter/unt_student_health_price_comparison_spring_2016.pdf">medical services pricing comparison</a> and our current <a href="/sites/default/files/healthcenter/rx_price_comarision_spring_2016.pdf">pharmacy pricing comparison</a> to see how we compare on most often requested services.</p>

		<div>
			<h3>
				Is there a charge for a form to be completed at the Health Center?</h3>

			<p>
				The medical providers at the Student Health and Wellness Center often complete medical related forms for students who are studying abroad, need a physical for a work or volunteer program or who need documentation for special accommodations.&nbsp; Typically a fee of $10 is charged for completing these types of forms for students.</p>
