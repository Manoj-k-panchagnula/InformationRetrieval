<td height="400"><big></big>
<p><big>Established in 1992 as the professional development arm of the Department of Public Administration, the mission of the Center for Public Management (CPM) is to promote excellence and efficiency in public and nonprofit organizations.  To this end, four goals have guided CPM since its inception:</big></p>
<p><big>1. Establish and maintain relationships with agencies in local, state, and federal government that would benefit from CPM’s technical and training assistance.</big></p>
<p><big>2. Establish and maintain cooperative relationships with professional associations that serve managers in public and nonprofit organizations.</big></p>
<p><big>3. Establish and maintain cooperative relationships with other UNT centers and institutes that share an interest in serving public and nonprofit organizations.</big></p>
<p><big>4. Provide research and training opportunities to UNT faculty and graduate students.</big></p>
<p><big>The programs and services offered by CPM directly support UNT’s mission to assume a primary leadership role in addressing community needs of the region and the state.  CPM contributes to UNT's effort to cultivate partnerships with educational institutions at all levels, government agencies, businesses, and nonprofit organizations to improve the quality of education and serve as a source for lifelong learning and professional education.</big></p>
</td>
