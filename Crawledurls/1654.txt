                <h2 class="title" id="page-title">Information for Guests</h2>

  <div class="container-12">
  	    <div class="grid-3 content-area sidebar-content sidebar-right top-important"><div class="inside">
	      <div class="field field-name-related-links-for-guests-fall field-type-ds field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"> <div class="color-box bg-aqua">

	      <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded"> <p>Graduation is a time to celebrate, not just for graduates but also for the family and friends who helped them reach this milestone. UNT is proud to welcome you to share in the achievements of your UNT graduate.</p>
<h3>Fall Undergraduate College Commencement Ceremony</h3>
<p>Fall Undergraduate College Commencement Ceremonies are held every December to give graduates the opportunity to celebrate the more traditional moments of graduation. At these ceremonies, bachelor's degree candidates will be called by name and will walk the stage and shake hands with their college administration. Graduates wear their <a href="../regalia-overview">regalia</a>. The fall college commencement ceremonies include degree conferral.</p>
<p><a href="../fall-commencement-overview#undergradrecognition">Learn more about Fall Undergraduate College Recognition Ceremonies</a></p>
<h3>Fall Doctoral and Master's Commencement with Doctoral/M.F.A. Hooding</h3>
<p>Doctoral and Master's Commencement is held every December to recognize the accomplishments of students who completed post-graduate studies by August or December of that year. Doctoral and M.F.A. candidates (recipients of terminal degrees) are hooded during this ceremony by their faculty mentor. Graduates wear their <a href="../regalia-overview">regalia</a>. The fall doctoral and master’s commencement includes degree conferral.</p>
<p><a href="../fall-commencement-overview#doctoral">Learn more about Fall Doctoral and Master's Convocation</a>.</p>
<h3>University-wide Commencement</h3>
<p>Held every year in May, UNT's annual University-wide Commencement honors UNT's annual graduating class with pomp and circumstance, featuring an inspirational speaker, degree conferral and commencement celebrations. This event is for graduates within an academic year. Fall graduates are encouraged to return to campus the following spring to attend the University-Wide Commencement ceremony.</p>
<h3>Commencement Ceremony Details</h3>
<h4>University-wide Commencement</h4>
<ul><li>Formal processional into Apogee Stadium with graduates seated as directed on the stadium field. Please note: Graduates will be seated by the level of degree they earn (bachelor's, master's, doctoral), not by college or alphabetically.</li>
</ul><p>Degree conferral is when the president asks graduates to stand so they can be formally recognized by degree level. Because of the large number of graduates expected, graduates are not called by name, won't walk the stage or have their photo taken. This happens during the undergraduate college recognition commencement ceremonies and doctoral/master’s commencement.</p>
<p>Important Guest Information:</p>
<ul><li>Guests should arrive at least an hour before the event.</li>
<li>Guests will be seated in the stadium seats.</li>
<li>Guests are asked to stay for the duration of the ceremony.</li>
</ul><h4>Undergraduate and Graduate Ceremonies</h4>
<p>The Fall Undergraduate College Commencement Ceremonies and the Doctoral and Master's Commencement includes:</p>
<ul><li>Graduates being called by name, walking the stage, shaking hands with college administration and having their photo taken. Doctoral and M.F.A. candidates recipients of terminal degrees) will be hooded by their mentor professor(s). The fall ceremonies include degree conferral.</li>
</ul><p>Important Guest Information:</p>
<ul><li>Undergraduate College Commencement Ceremonies and the Doctoral and Master's Commencement are held in either the UNT Coliseum or the Murchison Performing Arts Center.</li>
<li>Doors to the Coliseum will open approximately one-and-a-half hours before the ceremony and guests should arrive about an hour before the event. Doors to the Margot and Bill Winspear Performance Hall inside the Murchison Performing Arts Center open a half-hour prior to the event, and guests should plan to arrive at about that time.</li>
<li>Guests are asked to stay for the duration of the ceremony.</li>
<li>While the typical commencement venues -- Apogee Stadium, UNT Coliseum and the Murchison Performing Arts Center -- are large venues, each ceremony does maximize seating capacity. So that guests in attendance can be seated and enjoy the event, the university requires that guests holding (saving) seats release them 10 minutes prior to each ceremony.</li>
<li>There is no admission charge and seating is not reserved.</li>
<li>For the college ceremonies and doctoral and master’s convocation, graduates can invite family and friends..</li>
<li>If graduation ceremonies reach seating capacity, guests will be directed to nearby venues to watch live broadcasts of the ceremonies on television screens. Due to fire codes and safety issues, there will be no standing room for guests.</li>
<li>ADA accessible seating is available for all venues is available upon request.</li>
</ul><h3>Photos</h3>
<p>Graduates will have their photo taken by professional photographers during the Fall Undergraduate College Commencement Ceremonies and the Doctoral and Master's Commencement. A posed photo will be taken with your diploma cover, and a candid shot will be taken as they shake hands with the university official. Proofs of these photos will be emailed from Flash Photography to the graduate’s UNT email address in the weeks following the ceremony.</p>
