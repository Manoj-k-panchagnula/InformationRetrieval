      <p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://engineering.unt.edu/mechanicalandenergy/sites/default/files/photos/Z0E.jpg" alt="Virtual Tour of Z0E Lab" width="516" height="291" /></p>
<p>The <span style="text-decoration: underline;">Z</span>er<span style="text-decoration: underline;">o</span> <span style="text-decoration: underline;">E</span>nergy (ZØE) Research Laboratory is a unique kind of building in Texas – designed specifically to test and demonstrate various alternative energy generation technologies in order to achieve a net-zero consumption of energy. The Net-zero energy philosophy is based on a combination of different renewable energy technologies in a building, such as solar, geothermal and wind systems, which leads to produce enough energy to power a building and in many cases even create excess energy to return back to the power grid and thus the net energy consumption over a period or year becomes zero.</p>
<p>The 1,200 square-foot facility will give UNT students a real-world training and research opportunities on the growing sustainable and renewable energy sector.</p>
<h2>Vision and Mission</h2>
<p>The Zero Energy Research laboratory at the University of North Texas will be one of the very few places for worldwide students to get hands-on experience on the green building technologies that will power the future. This facility will be a great resource for UNT students, researchers and other industry partners to conduct multi-faceted research program in Renewable Energy, Energy Conservation and sustainability design.</p>
<p>As ZØE is a research laboratory, there are vast opportunities for the researcher to conduct their research on diverse sectors.&nbsp; The mission of ZØE is to convey all the facilities for a researcher &amp; generate a path to develop the renewable energy sector for sustainable, cost effective, comfortable building applications</p>

<h2>ZØE at a Glance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Address:</strong> 3920 N. Elm St., Discovery Park Campus</li>
<li><strong>Floor area:</strong> 1200 square feet</li>
<li>Open flexible work/laboratory space and an attached covered loading/work shop area</li>
<li>A living quarter with a bathroom &amp; a small kitchen with refrigerator</li>
<li>Steel column/beams, brick façade/SIP panels/glass</li>
<li>Structural insulated panels (SIP) for walls/roof</li>
<li>Centered utility core for easy operation and remodeling</li>
<li>Advanced energy efficient cooling and heating systems</li>
<li>Sustainability features include bamboo flooring and millwork, local materials, recycled glass countertop and backsplash, rain-harvesting water system, and renewable energy power (solar and wind)</li>
</ul>
