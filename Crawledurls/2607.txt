<div class="inner">
<h3>How Departments Can Participate</h3>
<p>Departments interested in participating in the Work Study program should download the materials and the participation agreements. Please scan and email signed agreements to <a href="mailto:collegeworkstudy@unt.edu">collegeworkstudy@unt.edu</a> . If you have any questions, please send an email to <a href="mailto:collegeworkstudy@unt.edu">collegeworkstudy@unt.edu</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/sites/default/files/workstudy/2016%20FEDERAL%20WORK%20STUDY%20MANUAL.pdf">Federal Work Study Manual</a></li>
<li><a href="/sites/default/files/workstudy/15-16%20FED%20WS%20PART%20AGREEMENT.pdf">Federal Work Study Participation Agreement</a></li>
<li><a href="/sites/default/files/workstudy/15-16%20%20TX%20WS%20PART%20AGREEMENT.pdf">Texas Work Study Participation Agreement</a></li>
</ul>
<p>The previous documents require the free <a href="https://get.adobe.com/reader/">Adobe Acrobat Reader</a>.</p>
</div>
</div>
<p>Work Study jobs are partially funded by the state or federal government. Your eligibility for the work-study program is determined by financial need, satisfactory academic progress, and an enrollment status of half time or more.</p>
<p>If you're eligible, you may go to the <a href="http://careercenter.unt.edu/">UNT Career Center</a> to learn about available jobs. Typical positions include office assistant, library assistant, cafeteria helper and lab assistant.</p>
<h3>Eligibility Criteria</h3>
<p>Below is a list of eligibility requirements. You must:</p>
<ul>
<li>Show financial need.</li>
<li>Be enrolled at least half-time.</li>
<li>Meet <a href="../satisfactory-academic-progress-requirements">Satisfactory Academic Progress Requirements</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3>How to Apply</h3>
<p>Complete a Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) or Renewal Application. Indicate your preference for College Work Study on the FAFSA.</p>
<p>If you're eligible, the Student Financial Aid and Scholarships office will notify you through MyUNT, where you may accept the Work-Study award. Thereafter, you can contact the <a href="http://studentaffairs.unt.edu/career-center">UNT Career Center</a> for available jobs.</p>
<h4>UNT Priority Dates</h4>
<ul>
<li>Apply early because funds are limited</li>
<li>Applications considered on the basis of available funds after the priority dates. Review the <a href="../priority-dates-and-deadlines">priority dates</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Continued Eligibility</h4>
<p>In order to remain eligible for the program, you must:</p>
<ul>
<li>Show financial need.</li>
<li>Be enrolled at least half-time.</li>
<li>Continue to meet <a href="../satisfactory-academic-progress-requirements">Satisfactory Academic Progress Requirements</a></li>
</ul>
<h4>Amount of Awards</h4>
<ul>
<li>Based on established financial need.</li>
<li>Maximum award is $4,000 per nine-month academic period.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Payment</h4>
<ul>
<li>Students are generally paid on the 1st and 15th of every month.</li>
<li>Students are paid at least the federal minimum wage.</li>
<li>Payment is based on the number of hours worked during previous month.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Repayment</h4>
<ul>
<li>Repayment is not required. The money you earn is yours to help pay your education expenses.</li>
<li>Must be reported as earnings on your income tax return.</li>
<li>When completing the FAFSA for the following year, report your Work-Study earnings under the Additional Financial Information section. (Work-Study earnings are treated as financial aid rather than income when the Expected Family Contribution is calculated.)</li>
</ul>  </div>
