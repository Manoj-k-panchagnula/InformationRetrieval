<h3>Put Out Tobacco!</h3>
<p><strong><em>Do you know the Tobacco Grade for the State of Texas?</em></strong></p>
<p>In an effort to reduce the adverse effects caused by tobacco use, each state and the District of Columbia are now graded on the effectiveness of their tobacco control laws utilizing measures established by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). The highest attainable score is 55. This is equivalent to an &ldquo;A&rdquo;. Texas scored a 9; earning an &ldquo;F&rdquo;.</p>
<p><strong>Texas still earns an &ldquo;F&rdquo; in Tobacco Prevention and Smoke-free air, but Texas gets recognition for increasing funding over $5 million for tobacco prevention and cessation programs in 2014!</strong></p>
<p>Find out more at:&nbsp;<a href="http://www.stateoftobaccocontrol.org/state-grades/texas/">http://www.stateoftobaccocontrol.org/state-grades/texas/</a></p>
<p>Can you guess who earned the best grade for prevention and Smoke-free air?</p>
<p>Visit <a href="http://www.stateoftobaccocontrol.org/state-grades/north-dakota/"><em>Going above and beyond</em></a> to find out!</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
</div>
<div class="box">
<h3>Did you know...</h3>
<p>Thomas Edison failed 10,000 times at inventing the light bulb and James Dyson went through 5,126 prototypes before arriving at his &ldquo;revolutionary vacuum cleaner.&rdquo; The product WD-40 literally means &ldquo;Water Displacement&mdash;40th Attempt.&rdquo; None of these people gave up! Instead, they kept a clear goal in mind. We want our students to do the same and we are here to provide resources to help you to <a href="https://success.unt.edu/succeed-at-unt?utm_source=StudentSuccessCamUNTHome&amp;utm_medium=web&amp;utm_campaign=StudentSuccess2013">Succeed at UNT!</a> Want additional resources? <a href="http://studentwellness.unt.edu/about">Click here</a></p>
</div>
<div class="box">
<h3>Eating healthy, no matter the season</h3>
<p><span style="background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);">The cold winter months can make it tough to eat many fruits and veggies. Focus on seasonal options, like broccoli, carrots, dark leafy greens, sweet potatoes and citrus. For a complete list of Texas produce available during each season, check out the&nbsp;</span><a href="http://gotexan.org/Portals/1/doc/pdf/publications/produce_avail_050310.pdf" style="-webkit-transition: all 0.1s linear; transition: all 0.1s linear; color: rgb(0, 133, 62); text-decoration: none; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);">Go Texan Produce Availability</a><span style="background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);">&nbsp;chart. Don&rsquo;t forget that canned, frozen or dried produce can also be a great option, but try to choose items with little or no added sugar, salt or fat.</span></p>
</div>
<div class="box">
<h3>Nutrition Resources for College Students</h3>
<p>Did you know that UNT had a registered dietitian on staff that is available for nutritional consultations? To schedule an appointment, call 940-565-2333. <a href="http://healthcenter.unt.edu/sites/default/files/Ask%20a%20Dietitian%20Sign.pdf" target="_blank" title="Ask a Dietician">Click here for appointment prices.</a></p>
<p><strong>Online Resources</strong> include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://www.choosemyplate.gov" title="choosemyplate.gov">ChooseMyPlate.gov</a> which has great resources for everyone- but especially for <a href="http://www.choosemyplate.gov/MyPlateOnCampus/index.html" title="MyPlateOnCampus">college students</a>! It also has <a href="http://www.choosemyplate.gov/MyPlateOnCampus/Recipes.html" title="MyPlateOnCampus/Recipes">recipes</a>, and other <a href="http://www.choosemyplate.gov/MyPlateOnCampus/Resources.html" title="MyPlateOnCampus/Resources">tips and handouts</a> for students.</li>
<li><a href="http://readsh101.com/unt.html" title="Student Health 101 monthly UNT edition">Student Health 101&mdash;an online </a>magazine with recipe ideas and other ways to stay healthy and connected at UNT.</li>
<li>Student Health and Wellness Center <a href="https://www.facebook.com/untSHWC?ref=hl" title="facebook.com/untSHWC">Facebook page</a> from time to time as the dietitian will post healthy nutrition tips and you can find weekly information about nutrition related programs and all our other health education programs and events.</li>
</ul>
