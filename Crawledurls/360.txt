			<h2 class="title" id="page-title">How do I apply?</h2>
			
			        
                <div class="block block-system block-main block-system-main odd block-without-title" id="block-system-main">
  <div class="block-inner clearfix">
                
    <div class="content clearfix">
      <div  about="/transfer/how-to-apply" typeof="sioc:Item foaf:Document" id="node-page-176" class="ds-1col node node-page view-mode-full node-not-promoted node-not-sticky author-wdc odd clearfix clearfix">

  
  <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded"><h3 class="sub-title">How and when to apply for admission to UNT as a transfer student</h3>

<h3>Who can apply as a transfer student?</h3>
<p>U.S. citizens or U.S. permanent residents applying for admission to UNT for the first time, who have earned college credits from another school of higher education after graduating high school and have not earned a four-year degree should apply as transfer students.</p>
<p><strong>Not you? Try one of these links:</strong> <a href="/freshman/how-to-apply">Freshman</a> | <a href="/international/how-to-apply">International</a> | <a href="http://tsgs.unt.edu/future-students/graduate-admissions">Graduate</a></p>
<hr />
<a id="how-apply"></a>
<h3>How to apply</h3>
<p>To be considered for admission to UNT, do the following:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong><a href="https://www.applytexas.org" target="_blank" title="Online Admission Application at ApplyTexas.org (opens in a new window)">Complete the online application at ApplyTexas.org</a></strong> before the <a href="#when-to-apply">priority date</a>.</li>
  <li><strong><a href="#application-fee">Pay the UNT application fee</a></strong> before the <a href="#when-to-apply">priority date</a>.</li>  <li><strong><a href="#transcripts">Submit separate, official transcripts</a></strong> from every university and college you&rsquo;ve attended. If you've earned fewer than 30 college credit hours, also submit an official transcript from your high school.</li>
  <li><strong>Submit official SAT or ACT scores</a></strong> if you have fewer than 30 college credit hours. You do not have to send test scores if you have 30 or more college credit hours. If you’ve been out of high school for five years or longer, have earned fewer than 30 college credit hours and have not taken the SAT or ACT, you may qualify for <a href="http://admissions.unt.edu/adult-admission-program">Adult Admission</a>.</li>

</ul>

<p><a href="/international/how-to-apply">International students will have additional application requirements.</a></p>
<a id="mail"></a>
<h4>If you are submitting documents or payments by mail...</h4>
<p><strong>Our regular mailing address is:</strong><br>
  University of North Texas<br>
  Office of Undergraduate Admissions<br>
  1155 Union Circle #311277<br>
  Denton, TX 76203-5017</p>
<p><strong>Our physical address for courier services (FedEx, UPS, etc.) is:</strong><br>
  University of North Texas<br>
  Office of Undergraduate Admissions<br>
  Eagle Student Services Center Room 305<br>
  1147 Union Circle<br>
  Denton, TX 76203</p>
<hr />

<a id="when-to-apply"></a>

<h3>When to Apply</h3>
<h4>Priority admission dates for transfer students:</h4>

<p>Submit your <a href="https://www.applytexas.org" target="_blank" title="Online Admission Application at ApplyTexas.org (opens in a new window)">completed application</a> and <a href="#application-fee">application fee</a> to the admissions office by the following priority dates.</p>

<table>
  <table>
  <tbody>
    <tr>
      <th>Apply by this priority date:</th>
      <th>To enroll for session:</th>
      <th>With classes beginning:</th>
    </tr>

      <td>Summer 2016 3-week session 1</td>
      <td>May 16, 2016</td>
    </tr>

      <td>August 29, 2016</td>
    </tr>
  </tbody>
</table>

<p><strong><span class="red-star">*</span>You should apply and be admitted by March 1 to be considered for scholarships.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Applications received after these priority dates will still be considered, but will be subject to a <a href="#application-fee">late fee</a>.</strong></p>

<p>Your application and fee must be submitted by the priority date for you to be eligible for on-time registration. Applicants who miss the priority date will register during Late Registration when classes are less plentiful.</p>


<p><a href="/freshman/how-to-apply" title="UNT admissions information for freshmen">Different admissions deadlines exist for new college students.</a></p>
<h4><strong>How early should you apply?</strong></h4>
<p>Transfer applicants may apply up to a year in advance of their expected term of enrollment. It's best to apply as early as possible so that you'll have more scholarships available, and more options for orientation dates and housing preferences.</p>
<h4>When are admission decisions made?</h4>
<p>Admission decisions are made on a rolling basis. You'll be informed of your admission status once we've received and processed your complete application and all required documents.</p>
<p><a href="/transfer/application-review-process" title="UNT transfer admission application review process and how to check application status">Learn more about our application review process and how to check the status of your application.</a></p>


<hr />

<a id="application-fee"></a>


<h3>How to pay the application fee</h3>
<p>The application fee for U.S. Citizens &amp; Permanent residents is <strong>$75</strong>, if received by the Priority Date. After the Priority Date, the application fee is <strong>$90</strong>.</p>

<h4>Ways to pay</h4>
<p>You may pay the application fee by credit card, money order or check.</p>
<p>If paying by credit card, please include credit card information when completing your application for admission or call 1-800-UNT-8211 (1-800-868-8211) to pay by phone.</p>
<p>If paying by money order or check, please mail your payment to the UNT Office of Undergraduate Admissions. <a href="#mail">See our mailing address.</a></p>
<p>Please include your name on your money order or check so we can credit the payment to your account.</p>
<p>The application fee must be paid in U.S. dollars and is non-refundable.</p>

<h4>Fee waivers</h4>
<p>In limited cases, the admission application fee may be waived.</p>
<p>As a transfer applicant, you may request an admission application fee waiver by sending us a copy of your Student Aid Report (SAR) from your Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA). In order to be considered for the waiver, your Estimated Family Contribution from the SAR must be zero.</p>


<hr />

<a id="transcripts"></a>

<h3>How to submit transcripts</h3>

<p>Please send your transcripts to the UNT Office of Undergraduate Admissions. You can send them via regular mail, have them delivered by a courier service (UPS, FedEx, etc.), or have them sent from your school via an electronic transcript service. You can also hand-deliver them to the UNT Office of Undergraduate Admissions. <a href="#mail">See our mailing address.</a></p>


<hr />
<h3>Letters of recommendation and essays</h3>
<p>You will not need to mail letters of recommendation and an essay (personal statement) with your application unless additional information is requested of you by an admission officer and is necessary to evaluate your application for admission to the university.</p>
<p>If letters of recommendation and an essay are required, you will receive a letter from an admission officer requesting those documents. At that time, you should submit your essay and ask the appropriate people to submit letters of recommendation directly to our office. Letters of recommendation should be submitted by your your advisor and two or three professors.</p>
