<h2>Texas Governor Greg Abbott</h2>

<div style="margin: 5px 10px 5px 5px; width: 320px; float: left;"><a href="/multimedia/photos/2015-GovernorAbbott-Portrait_HiRes.jpg" target="_blank"><img alt="Texas Governor Gregg Abbott" src="/multimedia/photos/2015-GovernorAbbott-Portrait.jpg" style="padding: 4px; border: 1px solid rgb(207, 197, 175); margin-bottom: 7px; background-color: rgb(235, 229, 216);" title="Texas Governor Greg Abbott" /></a></div>

<p>Elected to serve as the 48th Governor of State of Texas, Greg Abbott has a strong record of conservative leadership as Texas&rsquo; longest-serving Attorney General.</p>

<p>A husband and father, Governor Abbott has spent his career fighting to preserve Texas values like faith, family and freedom for future generations. As the state&rsquo;s chief law enforcement official, Governor Abbott made protecting Texas communities the focus of his tenure as Texas&rsquo; longest-serving Attorney General.</p>

<p>Shortly after taking office as Attorney General, Governor Abbott established a Cyber Crimes Unit to arrest criminals who use the Internet to prey upon children; a Fugitive Unit to arrest convicted sex offenders who violate their parole; and an expanded Medicaid Fraud Control Unit to crack down on elder abuse and waste of taxpayer dollars. During his time serving as Attorney General, he has collected more than $31 billion in child support for Texas children. In 2014, his office was recognized as the nation&rsquo;s best child support program by the National Child Support Enforcement Association.</p>

<p>As a candidate for governor, Governor Abbott cast a vision to build a better future for Texas, a vision focused on creating jobs and economic opportunity, improving education, building transportation infrastructure and securing Texas&rsquo; border in the face of federal government inaction.</p>

<p>Prior to serving as the 50th Attorney General of Texas, Governor Greg Abbott served as a Justice on the Texas Supreme Court and as a State District Judge in Harris County.</p>

<p>A native Texan, Governor Abbott was born in Wichita Falls and raised in Duncanville. After graduating from The University of Texas at Austin with a B.B.A. in Finance, he received his law degree from Vanderbilt University Law School. Shortly after graduating from law school, he was partially paralyzed by a falling tree while jogging.</p>

<p>Governor Abbott and his wife, First Lady Cecilia Abbott, a former school teacher and principal, have been married for 33 years. They live in Austin with their daughter, Audrey. He is an avid sportsman and hunter.</p>
