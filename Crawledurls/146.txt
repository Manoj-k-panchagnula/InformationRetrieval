<h3><font color="green">Google Chrome drops NPAPI support (Java)</font></h3>
<p style="margin-bottom:0;">As of Google Chrome 42, support for NPAPI plug-ins will no longer be enabled by default.  Popular NPAPI plug-ins include <b>Java</b> and <b>Silverlight</b>, amongst others.</p>
<p>If you use Google Chrome, the Helpdesk recommends you follow Oracle's instruction to re-enable NPAPI support, until Oracle releases another option.  This will ensure you can use all Java based features in <b>Blackboard</b>.  Instructions may be found here:
<ul style="padding-top:-40px;">
	<li><a target="_blank" href="https://www.java.com/en/download/faq/chrome.xml#npapichrome">https://www.java.com/en/download/faq/chrome.xml#npapichrome</a>.</li>
</ul>
<br />Alternatively, you may switch to another browser that does support NPAPI plug-ins, such as <a href="www.firefox.com">Firefox</a>, <a href="https://support.apple.com/downloads/#safari">Safari</a>, or <a href="http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/internet-explorer/download-ie">Internet Explorer</a>.
</p>
<br />

<h3>Where are my classes?</h3>

Classes are generally not available within Blackboard Learn until the first official day of class. 

<ul class="disk">

	<li>If you just registered or added a class after the semester started, it may take up to 24 hours for your course to appear</li>

	<li>If you previously registered, and your course is not listed, your professor may not have made that course available yet.  You can contact your instructor to verify this.</li>

	<li>If you still have concerns regarding a missing course you can contact the UIT Helpdesk, and we will verify your enrollment and course visibility.</li>

</ul>
