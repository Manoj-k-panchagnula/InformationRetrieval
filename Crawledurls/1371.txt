<p style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><strong>Overview</strong></p>
<p style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">Wilderness First Responder (WFR) is the definitive course in medical training for outdoor educators, guides, SAR team members, and others who work or play in remote areas. The curriculum is comprehensive and practical, including all of the essential principles and skills required to assess and manage medical problems in isolated and extreme environments. The 5-day format is for individuals and sponsors with less time available for on-site training and requires 25 hours of pre-course preparation.</p>
<p style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">All students must complete all pre-course work prior to arrival on course.</p>
<h3 style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><strong>Why Wilderness Medical Associates International?</strong></h3>
<ul style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
<li style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><strong>Curriculum</strong>. Based on nearly three decades of oversight and continual review by a faculty committee of medical practitioners, our curriculum is established, proven and is based on conceptual learning and critical thinking.</li>
<li style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><strong>Instructors</strong> are medical professionals who are in the field doing what they teach. They are required to complete rigorous training and bring an unprecedented amount of experience to the courses they teach.</li>
<li style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><strong>Courses</strong> devote significant time to practical sessions and realistic rescue simulations that prepare students for the stress of actual emergency situations in the field.</li>
<li style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><strong>Recognition</strong>. Teaching over 8,000 students annually, and having been teaching Wilderness First Responder and Wilderness EMT courses since the 1980's, we are the "standard by which all other wilderness medical providers are judged."</li>
</ul>
<h3 style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><strong>Description</strong></h3>
<p style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">25 Hours Pre-Course Preparation and 45 Hours On-Site Training</p>
<h3 style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><strong>Class Format</strong></h3>
<p style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">This course covers the same material and meets the same criteria as our standard Wilderness First Responder. It is designed for students and professionals who have tight schedules and the self-discipline to complete approximately 25 hours of pre-course preparation.</p>
<p style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br />Students will have access to the WFR Study Guide two to four weeks before the course starts. They are required to complete the assigned reading, case studies, study guide, and tests. The on-site instruction that follows reinforces and expands on the knowledge already acquired. Because students arrive familiar with the didactic material, on-site daytime training can focus on wilderness and rescue scenarios and practical skills. Evenings are reserved for study and assignments. Expect rescue scenarios with made-up victims and simulated wounds to prepare you for backcountry emergencies. Sessions may be videotaped for enhanced learning.</p>
<p style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br />The 5-Day WFR course has been through five years of development and testing. Conscientious preparation, studying, and time prior to the course is essential for success. This is not a short-cut version of our standard course. Established WMA International clients and other groups who are interested in this new format are encouraged to contact us about this offering.</p>
<h3 style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><strong>Textbooks</strong></h3>
<p style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">The following teaching materials are provided by WMA International at no additional cost:</p>
<ul style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
<li style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><a href="http://www.wildmed.com/blog/book-store/wilderness-and-rescue-medicine-warm/" target="_blank">Wilderness and Rescue Medicine: A guide for the basic and advanced practitioner</a></li>
<li style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><a href="http://www.wildmed.com/blog/book-store/wilderness-and-rescue-medicine-workbook/">Wilderness Medicine Workbook</a></li>
<li style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><a href="http://www.wildmed.com/blog/book-store/the-field-guide-of-wilderness-rescue-medicine/" target="_blank">The Field Guide of Wilderness &amp; Rescue Medicine</a></li>
<li style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">WFR Class Notes and Handouts</li>
<li style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">SOAP Notes</li>
</ul>
<h3 style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><strong>Completion and Grades</strong></h3>
<p style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">Successful completion with certification is based on 100% attendance, satisfactory performance on homework assignments and written quizzes, demonstrated proficiency with practical skills and a grade of 80% or better on final written exam. WMA International is committed to making reasonable accommodation to any student with special needs.</p>
<h3 style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><strong>Certification</strong></h3>
<p style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">All eligible students who successfully complete the course will receive Wilderness First Responder, Anaphylaxis, and Healthcare Provider level CPR certifications. The CPR course is based on the 2010 ILCOR/AHA guidelines. All WMA certifications remain valid for three years.</p>
<h3 style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><strong>Prerequisites</strong></h3>
<p style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">Students must be at least 18 years old to participate in the 5-Day WFR course. Certain course sponsors may set a higher minimum age or establish other requirements consistent with their program. No previous medical training is required.</p>
<h3 style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><strong>Syllabus</strong></h3>
<ul style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
<li style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">General Concepts in Wilderness and Rescue Medicine</li>
<li style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">Patient Assessment System Critical Body Systems: BLS and CPR; Anaphylaxis and Asthma</li>
<li style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">Practical Skills: Lifting, Moving and Extrication; Spine Stabilization and Litter Packaging; Wound Cleaning and Exploration</li>
<li style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">Environmental Topics: Exposure Control; Bites and Stings; Altitude Illness; Cold Injuries; Diving Emergencies; Thermoregulation; Lightning; Near Drowning; Avalanche</li>
<li style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">Musculoskeletal Systems: Spine Injury Assessment; Musculoskeletal Injuries; Extremity Splinting; Dislocations</li>
<li style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">Other: Medical Legal Issues; Soft Tissue Injury; Toxins; Search and Rescue; Backcountry Medicine; Emergency Childbirth; Medical Kits</li>
</ul>
