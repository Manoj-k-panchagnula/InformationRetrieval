	<strong>The White House Emerging Technologies Committee Lays Out Principles for Guidance</strong></p>
<p>
	The White House Emerging Technologies Interagency Policy Coordination Committee (ETIPC) has released a memorandum (<a href="http://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/default/files/microsites/ostp/etipc-memo-3-11-2011.pdf">http://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/default/files/microsites/ostp/etipc-memo-3-11-2011.pdf</a>) &nbsp;to the heads of executive departments and agencies outlining broad principles to guide the development and implementation of policies for oversight of emerging technologies at the agency level.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>
	The Principles reflect the Committee&rsquo;s goal of striking a balance in which novel technologies are subject to oversight that is adequate to protect public health and the environment but not so daunting as to unduly slow innovation or the development of those new technologies.</p>
<p class="rtecenter">

	<span class="Apple-style-span" style="color: rgb(178, 34, 34); font-size: 16px; "><strong>The Showcase@WBT</strong></span></p>
<p style="margin-top: 0px; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; margin-left: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-left: 0px; ">
	<b>World class mentoring for emerging technologies...by investors and licensees, for investors and licensees&nbsp;</b></p>
<p>	The Showcase represents up to 100 companies and technologies selected to present products or technologies that are market-ready and seeking startup, seed stage/first round capital, or corporate licensing partners.&nbsp;</p>
<p>	The WBT sources, screens, and mentors companies and technologies emerging from universities, federal labs, and the private sector. These companies and technologies, which vary by industry sector, geography, funding source, and type of research institution, are vetted by a national screening panel of over 80 venture investors and Fortune 1000 licensees. All selected presenters are matched with a two person mentor team in preparation for a concise, 6-minute investor/licensee-oriented presentation.&nbsp;</p>
<p>	<b>The deadline to submit a technology or company to be a part of the Showcase is in December</b>, and the 100 technologies are announced in late January. Over the past 8 years the WBT has worked with over 500 companies and technologies preparing them for venture capital and licensing partners.</p>

	<strong>The Texas Emerging Technology Fund</strong><br />
	<strong>North Texas Regional Center for Innovation and Commercialization (NTXRCIC)</strong></p>
<p>
	&nbsp;</p>
<p>
	The Texas Emerging Technology Fund (ETF) was established as an investment fund for technology-based start-up ventures with the objective of fostering research initiatives among the state&#39;s institutions of higher learning and encourage the development of &quot;next generation&quot; innovative, competitive technology companies in the State of Texas. The ETF is for Texas-based companies that will commercialize a breakthrough technology.</p>
<p>
	The North Texas Regional Center for Innovation &amp; Commercialization (NTXRCIC) is the regional agent for the North Texas area that identifies vets and coaches ETF applicants from 63 counties and an estimated population of 7.5 million.&nbsp;</p>
<p>
	For more information on the ETF grant please visit&nbsp; <a href="http://ntxrcic.org/webs/ntxrcic/default.aspx#list=M|1254">http://ntxrcic.org/webs/ntxrcic/default.aspx#list=M|1254</a></p>

For those who are new to incubation, the conference provides an opportunity to learn the fundamentals of business incubation and interact with others in the industry.
