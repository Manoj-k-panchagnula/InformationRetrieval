				<h1>
					About</h1>

				<p>
					Eaglethon is a year-long fundraising program that ends with the main event, a 6 hour long dance and activities to celebrate the children helped by Children&#39;s Miracle Network Hospitals each year. Some of the Cook Children&#39;s families participate in the event, and we highlight the kids to the fullest. This year&#39;s Eaglethon will be held on February 27 from 11am-6pm in the Union Ballrooms.</p>

						<h1>
							Make Miracles</h1>

						<p>
							On &quot;The BIG Day&quot;, Miracle Makers (Participants) &nbsp;remain active by dancing, standing or participating in various activities for the full 6 hours! Live entertainment and a DJ help pass the time, along with performances by local bands and university dancing and singing groups, and appearances from some UNT favorites!</p>

						<p>
							Many children and families give speeches about their struggles and the successes they have found because of the amazing hospital service.</p>

				<h1>
					Join a Committee</h1>

				<p>
					Eaglethon&rsquo;s success relies on passionate members who help with event planning and execution, fundraising, recruitment, and other programming throughout the year leading up to the man event.</p>

				<p>
					&nbsp;</p>

				<div class="row">
					<div class="col-md-4">
						<h2>
							Team Liaison</h2>

						<p>
							Liaisons are responsible for going to organizations meetings to help recruit teams as well as keeping teams up to date on all Eaglethon events leading up to the main event, Dance Marathon.</p>

						<p>
							Liaisons are needed from Panhellenic Council, Interfraternity Council, Multicultural Greek Council, National Pan-Hellenic Council, Residence Halls, Service Organizations, Academic Organizations, and general UNT organizations.</p>

						<p>
							Applicants should be a part of or joining at least one of the organizations listed. This is a great position for past team captains or participants who want to get more involved.</p>
					</div>

					<div class="col-md-4">
						<h2>
							General Committee</h2>

						<p>
							Members are really the bread and butter of Eaglethon because they allow all the programs we put on for the kids to be possible. These individuals have the most scheduling flexibility but are responsible for attending all meetings and events as needed. Great for go getters that love active volunteer work and having fun!</p>
					</div>

					<div class="col-md-4">
						<h2>
							Morale Team</h2>

						<p>
							The Morale team is a sub-committee of the general committee. These individuals are responsible for maintaining energy and optimism during the year and especially during the main event. Morale team are the cheerleaders for Eaglethon and may be asked to perform at things such Yell Like Hell, Tailgates, miscellaneous philanthropy events, and Eagle Escapades.</p>

						<p>
							Practices will be held before, during, or after general meeting so there is a slight time commitment. Great for energetic spunky individuals who aren&rsquo;t afraid to preform and show their passion for the kids.</p>
					</div>

				<p>
					Applications are available each March for the Eaglethon Executive Board. Meet our 2015-2016 Board.</p>

				<div class="row">

						<h2>
							Jessica and Jordan</h2>
						<strong>Executive Director</strong>

						<p>
							The Executive Director is responsible for overseeing all the other Directors and Members of Eaglethon. This individual should be available to all the other positions when needed as well as attending meetings with advisors, department heads, and other leaders on campus. This person will be responsible for making the meeting agendas, sending out emails to members, as well as monitoring the leadership facebook page. This individual needs to have good time management, be driven, and be good at delegating tasks. This is a great position for someone looking to advance their leadership skills on campus while spearheading a philanthropic movement.</p>
					</div>

					<div class="col-md-6">
						<h2>
							Carol and Aubrie</h2>

						<p>
							<strong>Directors of Programming</strong></p>

						<p>
							The Director of Programming is centered on recruiting and working with the miracle families and the day of Eaglethon. This includes the Opening and Closing activities, the day of show schedule, the day of show scripts, the slideshow for the miracle families, the set up for the day of Eaglethon, games/activities, instructions, and needs of the event. They will also be responsible for helping host the families while they are there at the event, and getting the families involved through the year by sending emails and invitations to the hospital.</p>

							The Director of Dancer Relations and Morale is responsible for overseeing the recruitment and retention of the dancers and members of Eaglethon. This includes upholding the morale of the executive board and committee, planning and leading icebreakers, helping out with retreats, creating and teaching the morale dance to the Eaglethon members and dancers, and overseeing Morale special events like Yell like Hell and at the day of Eaglethon. As well as, overseeing the recruitment of dancers, from the initial contact to the day of event, and passing along information to dancers about upcoming events and opportunities to receive points attributed to one of three cups (awards).</p>
					</div>

						<h2>
							Max</h2>
						<strong>Director of Fundraising and Sponsorship</strong>

						<p>
							The Director of Fundraising and Sponsorship is responsible with planning Eaglethon fundraising events and recruiting sponsors throughout the year. This includes creating and implementing Dine-in nights at restaurants, planning with the Director of Dancer Relations and Morale fundraising point options for the Miracle Cup(s), planning with Executive Director fundraising events to help Eaglethon meet the fundraising goals, finding sponsors for dancer giveaways, donations (monetary, silent auction, in kind, food, etc for the event), and other sponsorship opportunities.</p>
