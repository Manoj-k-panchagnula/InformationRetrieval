        <h1>Credit by Examination</h1>

        <br />

        <p>Are you in high school but want to get a head start
on earning your bachelor's degree at the University
of North Texas? There are several ways for you to
accomplish this even before enrolling in your first class
as an incoming freshman.</p>
		<p>By completing Advanced Placement (AP) courses
and exams, College-Level Examination Program (CLEP)
subject exams or International Baccalaureate (IB) exams,
you can earn academic credit and test out of some UNT
courses. This, in turn, helps you earn your degree sooner
and start your career earlier than other students.</p>
		<p>These courses and exams can be taken during high
school, at another college or university or once you
enroll at UNT.</p>
		<p>AP exams, necessary scores and equivalent courses
are listed in the next section. (Information is subject to change.) CLEP and IB exams
are reviewed by the dean who oversees your major to
determine how they can apply to your degree. Credit
by exam may not be:</p>
		<ul>
        <li>Used to reduce certain general requirements, such
as completing 30 credit hours at UNT</li>
		<li>Granted for a prerequisite course</li>
		<li>Substituted for any grade (including an F) that
you earned for a course in high school, at UNT
or at another college or university</li>
		</ul>
        <p>The credit you earn from an exam won't be
          included in your GPA, but it'll be included in your
          accepted credit hours and on your UNT transcript.</p>
		<h2>AP exams</h2>
		<p>You'll need to complete an AP course offered by
your high school to take an AP exam. Most Texas school
districts offer the exams in May. Homeschooled students
or students whose schools don't offer AP courses aren't
required to complete an AP course before taking an exam.</p>
		<p>Here's a breakdown of available AP courses and
exams, the credit hours that can be earned and our
course equivalent.</p>

						<h2>CLEP subject exams</h2>
		<p>These exams are scheduled throughout the year at
colleges and universities. We certify the credit if you score at
or above the average national CLEP score for that subject.</p>
		<p>A list of the CLEP subject exams you can take
and the credit hours that can be applied toward
your bachelor's degree is below. </p>
		<p>Exams administered at UNT are scheduled by
appointment only. To schedule a CLEP exam at UNT,
contact Counseling and Testing Services (located in the
Gateway Center, Room 140) at 940-369-7617. You can also
visit <a href="http://counselingandtesting.unt.edu">
Counseling and Testing Services</a> for more information.</p>
