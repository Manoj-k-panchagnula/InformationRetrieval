            <a name="copyright" id="copyright"></a>
            <h5>Copyright and Illegal Peer-to-Peer File Sharing</h5>
            <p> UNT takes copyright law very seriously and as such has developed policies prohibiting unauthorized duplication and distribution of copyrighted materials, including copyrighted music. Sanctions for violations of these policies include:</p>
            <ul>
              <li> loss of computing privileges</li>
              <li>fines</li>
              <li>dismissal from the University</li>
              <li> criminal or civil action</li>
            </ul>
            <p>UNT also employs a number of technical and procedural measures to prevent illegal downloading and distribution of copyrighted materials. A summary of these measures, including references to applicable policies, can be found on the <a href="http://security.unt.edu/node/1140">IT Shared Services website, UNT Approach to Addressing Copyright Infringement</a>. Legal alternatives to copyright infringing downloading can be found on the <a href="https://it.unt.edu/legaltour">University Information Technology website</a>.</p>
            <h5>Fire Safety Right to Know Report, Annual Disclosure of Fire Statistics</h5>

            <h5>Privacy of Student Records (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act)</h5>
            <p>The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) is a federal law that gives students the right to:</p>
            <ul>
              <li> review their records</li>
              <li>to request amendments to their records</li>
              <li>to consent to disclosures of personally identifiable information</li>
              <li>and to file complaints with the U.S. Department of Education</li>
            </ul>
            <p> <a href="http://www.unt.edu/ferpa/">Get additional information regarding student rights under FERPA</a>.</p>
            <a name="temp6" id="temp6"></a>
            <h5>FERPA</h5>
            <p>Pursuant to the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), the university has established policies relating to the accessibility of student information in the custody of the University of North Texas.</p>
<p>FERPA gives students basic rights with respect to their education record:</p>
<ul>
  <li>The right to control disclosure of their education record</li>
  <li>The right to review their education record</li>
  <li>The right to request amendment of inaccurate or misleading portions of their education record</li>
  <li>The right to file a complaint regarding non-compliance of FERPA with the Family Policy Compliance Office of the U.S. Department of Education</li>
</ul>
<p>The UNT FERPA policy statement appears in its entirety in the <a href="http://policy.unt.edu">UNT Policy Manual</a>, policy number 18.1.9.</p>
<a name="temp7" id="temp7"></a>
<h5>Transfer of Credit and Articulation</h5>
<p>Students who complete work at another institution, to be applied toward a bachelor's degree at the University of North Texas, should make sure that the appropriate officer of the other institution furnishes to the Office of Admissions or the Registrar's Office at the University of North Texas a complete official transcript of such work.</p>
<p>A student who successfully completes a 42- to 48-semester-credit-hour common core curriculum at a state-assisted institution of higher education in Texas may transfer as &quot;core complete&quot; to UNT. The student will receive academic credit for each of the courses transferred. The student will need to work with an academic advisor in the appropriate college or school academic advising office to determine if additional requirements will be necessary to satisfy the 42-hour UNT core. For more information, see the <a href="http://registrar.unt.edu/transfer-guides">UNT transfer articulation website</a>.<u> </u></p>

            <h5>Vaccination Policies</h5>
            <p>The UNT Student Immunization and Screening/Testing Policy statement appears in its entirety in the <a href="http://policy.unt.edu">UNT Policy Manual</a>, policy number 18.1.6.</p>
            <a name="temp3" id="temp3"></a>
            <p>All new students, including transfer students, must show evidence of receipt of an initial bacterial meningitis vaccination (meningococcal meningitis vaccine) dose or <strong>booster in the last five years and at least ten days prior to the first day of the semester in which the student initially enrolls</strong>. This law also applies to any student returning to school after a break in enrollment for a fall or spring semester. Even though the bacterial meningitis vaccination is the only required immunization, the University of North Texas Student Health and Wellness Center (SHWC) recommends that all students have current immunizations for polio, diphtheria, pertussis, tetanus, rubella, mumps, and measles. Consideration should also be given to vaccination against hepatitis A, hepatitis B, and varicella. Immunization requirements can be found at the <a href="http://healthcenter.unt.edu/immunization-requirements/">Health Center website</a>.</p>
