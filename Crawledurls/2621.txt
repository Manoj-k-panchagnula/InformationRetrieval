            <h4>What you need to know before the weather gets bad.</h4>
        <h4>&nbsp;</h4>
        <p>When university officials think weather conditions  pose a significant threat to the safety of students, faculty and staff who  drive to campus, UNT will delay its opening time, close early or close for the  day.</p>
<p>The university administration will make every attempt  to decide about the need for a delayed-opening or closure as early as  possible.</p>
<p><strong>Online classes</strong> taught via Blackboard Learn <strong>are  not affected</strong> by weather delays and closings, unless the course  instructor otherwise informs students.</p>
<h4>Check online, Facebook and local media for weather information</h4>
    <p>Sometimes the university remains open during  wintery weather conditions. When inclement weather conditions exist, visit <a href="http://www.unt.edu">www.unt.edu</a>, check  the <a href="http://www.facebook.com/northtexas">UNT Facebook page</a> or listen for weather details on  local media outlets before you begin driving to campus. You can also follow <a href="http://twitter.com/unteaglealert">@UNTEagleAlert</a> to get emergency updates in real time.</p>
      <p>Current  UNT students, faculty and staff should receive an <a href="http://www.unt.edu/eaglealert/"><strong>Eagle  Alert message</strong></a> notifying you that the university is closing. Before winter weather arrives, be sure your contact number is correct at <a href="http://my.unt.edu"><strong>my.unt.edu</strong></a><strong>.</strong></p>
      <p>If the weather conditions could make driving dangerous, please check local media reports or this web site before setting out for campus. <strong>Only delayed openings or closures are announced</strong>. If an announcement is not made, then the university will be open.</p>
        <h4>Midday  closures</h4>
        <p>The  university may close and cancel classes in the middle of the day if weather  conditions deteriorate. In those cases, in addition to the Eagle Alert, Facebook and Twitter messages, the university also will issue an <strong>Official Notice via Outlook</strong> and main telephone numbers for UNT  departments may be forwarded to the university's Broadcast Notification System.  The system will provide callers with up-to-date information.</p>
        <h4>Consecutive closures</h4>
        <p>If the university is closed for more than one day, you should check <a href="http://www.unt.edu">www.unt.edu</a> for the latest information.  The university also will post up-to-date information on our Facebook wall, tweet updates, provide the information to campus telephone operators, and inform news media.</p>
      <p><a href="http://emergency.unt.edu/"><strong>Watch videos and learn more about emergency preparedness &gt;&gt;</strong></a></p>

      <h4 class="green-header">Winter weather terms</h4>
        <ul class="gray">
          <li>It may be helpful to learn these weather terms. A few important terms are:</li>
          <li>A &quot;<strong>freezing rain advisory</strong>&quot; means that periods of freezing rain or freezing drizzle will cause travel difficulties.</li>
          <li>A <strong>&quot;winter storm watch&quot;</strong> means conditions are right for ice and snow to develop.</li>
          <li>An <strong>&quot;ice storm warning&quot;</strong> means severe winter weather conditions are expected or occurring. Significant amounts of ice accumulations will make travel extremely dangerous or impossible.</li>
          <li>A <strong>&quot;winter storm warning&quot;</strong> means that an ice storm or snowstorm is entering the area.</li>
          <li>A <strong>&quot;winter weather advisory&quot;</strong> indicates that winter weather conditions are  expected to cause significant inconveniences and hazardous driving.</li>
        </ul>
