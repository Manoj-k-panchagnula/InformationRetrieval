			<h2 class="title" id="page-title">How do I prepare?</h2>
			
			        
                <div class="block block-system block-main block-system-main odd block-without-title" id="block-system-main">
  <div class="block-inner clearfix">
                
    <div class="content clearfix">
      <div  about="/freshman/preparing-to-apply" typeof="sioc:Item foaf:Document" id="node-page-171" class="ds-1col node node-page view-mode-full node-not-promoted node-not-sticky author-wdc odd clearfix clearfix">

  
  <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded"><h3 class="sub-title">Improve your chances of being admitted and receiving financial awards</h3>

<h3>Talk to an Admissions Counselor</h3>

<p>Let one of our friendly UNT Admissions Counselors guide you every step of the way. We&rsquo;ll answer your questions and assist you through the admissions process. Call us, email us or come see us. We also have events in cities across the U.S. and visit lots of high schools, so there's a good chance we can come visit you, too.</p>

<p><a href="/contact-us">Find your UNT Admissions Counselor.</a></p>

<hr />

<h3>Attend admissions events to learn more about UNT</h3>

<h4>UNT Live!</h4>

<p>We bring UNT to you with this informative, free reception held in cities across the country. Our Admissions Counselors will give you information on UNT, the admissions process, housing, scholarships and financial aid. You can also meet with various departments during the event who can answer any questions you might have regarding your transition to UNT.</p>

<p><a href="/unt-live">Find out when UNT Live! will be in your area.</a></p>

<h4>UNT Preview</h4>

<p>This free campus event, held each Fall and Spring, is your opportunity to get a sneak peek at campus life and your future. Bring your family and your friends, and allow our students, faculty and staff to help you discover why UNT is the right place for you.</p>

<p><a href="https://untpreview.unt.edu" target="_blank" title="UNT Preview (opens in a new window)">Learn more about UNT Preview.</a></p>

<hr />
<h3>Tour campus</h3>

<p>Let our knowledgeable student Eagle Ambassadors take you on a tour of the beautiful UNT campus. Sign up for individualized tours of one of our colleges or schools and talk to a UNT Admissions Counselor in person.</p>

<p><a href="http://tours.unt.edu/" target="_blank" title="UNT Tours (opens in a new window)">Sign up for a campus tour.</a></p>

<hr />

<a id="courses"></a>

<h3>Take the right high school courses</h3>

<p>To be more successful at UNT, we require that students take the following classes in high school.</p>

<ul>

  <li>English: 4 credits (English I-IV)</li>

  <li>Mathematics: 4 credits (Algebra I, Geometry, Algebra II and Pre-Calculus)</li>

  <li>Sciences: 4 credits (to be selected from Biology, Chemistry, Environmental Sciences and Physics)</li>

  <li>Social Sciences: 4 credits (World Geography, World History Studies, U.S. Government and Economics and U.S. History)</li>

  <li>Foreign Language: 2 credits (Levels I-III proficiency of the same language) (UNT recommends 3 credits)</li>

  <li>Health: 1/2 credit minimum</li>

  <li>Fine Arts: 1 credit</li>

  <li>Physical Education: 1 1/2 credits</li>

  <li>Computer Science: 1 credit (demonstrated proficiency at Level I)</li>

  <li>Electives: 3 1/2 credits</li>

  <li>Speech: 1/2 credit</li>

</ul>

<hr />

<h3>Get a head start on your degree by earning college credits now</h3>

<p>You can get a head start on earning your bachelor’s degree at UNT even before enrolling in your first class as an incoming freshman. By completing Advanced Placement (AP) courses and exams, College-Level Examination Program (CLEP) subject exams or International Baccalaureate (IB) exams, you can earn academic credit and test out of some UNT courses. This can help you earn your degree faster and start your career earlier than other students.</p>

<p><a href="http://www.unt.edu/credit" target="_blank" title="UNT credit by examination information (opens in a new window)">Learn more about earning college credits by examination.</a></p>

<hr />

<h3>When to take the SAT or ACT</h3>

<p>High school seniors who plan to attend UNT should take entrance examinations at least five months before enrollment dates. See <a href="https://www.collegeboard.org/" title="CollegeBoard.org website (opens in a new window)" target="_blank">www.collegeboard.org</a> or your high school counselor for more information.</p>

<hr />

<h3>What to do your Junior year in high school</h3>

<h4>September</h4>

<ul>

  <li>Attend a college fair to begin gathering information on colleges.</li>

</ul>

<h4>October</h4>

<ul>

  <li>Take the PSAT.</li>

</ul>

<h4>January</h4>

<ul>

  <li>Take a free tour of campus. <a href="http://tours.unt.edu/"  target="_blank" title="UNT Tours (opens in a new window)">Schedule a campus tour online</a> or call 940-565-4104.</li>

</ul>

<h4>February</h4>

<ul>

  <li>Register for a spring SAT or ACT. Forward test results to UNT&rsquo;s Office of Undergraduate Admissions. <strong>UNT&rsquo;s school code is 6481 for the SAT and 4136 for the ACT.</strong></li>

  <li><a href="http://untpreview.unt.edu" target="_blank" title="UNT Preview (opens in a new window)">Attend UNT Spring Preview.</a></li>

</ul>

<h4>July</h4>

<ul>

  <li>Review requirements and deadlines for <a href="/freshman/how-to-apply">admission</a>, <a href="http://housing.unt.edu/housing_guide" target="_blank" title="UNT Housing Guide (opens in a new window)">housing</a>, <a href="/freshman/how-to-apply-for-scholarships-and-financial-aid">scholarships and financial aid</a>.</li>

</ul>

<hr />

<h3>What to do your Senior year in high school</h3>

<h4>August</h4>

<ul>

  <li><a href="/freshman/how-to-apply">Apply to UNT.</a></li>

  <li>Register for the fall SAT or ACT if necessary. Forward test results to UNT&rsquo;s Office of Undergraduate Admissions.</li>


</ul>

<h4>September</h4>

<ul>

  <li><a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/scholarships/apply-scholarships" target="_blank" title="How to apply for UNT scholarships (opens in a new window)">Submit the UNT General Scholarship Application.</a></li>

</ul>

<h4>October</h4>

<ul>

  <li>Remember to <a href="/freshman/how-to-apply#transcripts">send us your transcripts.</a></li>
  <li>Once you're accepted, <a href="http://studentaffairs.unt.edu/orientation#freshmen" target="_blank" title="UNT Freshman Orientation website (opens in a new window)">reserve a spot at Freshman Orientation.</a></li>


</ul>

<h4>January</h4>

<ul>

  <li><a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/finaidsteps" target="_blank" title="UNT financial aid information (opens in a new window)">Complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid</a> (FAFSA) and designate UNT as the college to receive your FAFSA information. <strong>UNT&rsquo;s school code is 003594</strong>.</li>

  <li><a href="http://housing.unt.edu/housing_guide" target="_blank" title="UNT Housing Guide (opens in a new window)">Reserve a residence hall at UNT</a> and include deposit and pre-payment.</li>


</ul>

<h4>April</h4>

<ul>

  <li><a href="http://premiere.unt.edu" target="_blank" title="UNT Premiere (opens in a new window)">Attend our UNT Premiere event for admitted students.</a></li>

</ul></div></div></div></div>
