    <p><strong><strong>Febuary 12, 2016 - PACCAR Research Forum Announcement</strong></strong></p>
<p>For a PDF version of this announcement please click this link: <a href="https://paccar.unt.edu/sites/default/files/PACCAR Research Forum 2016 Flyer.pdf" target="_blank">PACCAR Forum Flyer</a><a href="https://paccar.unt.edu/sites/default/files/PACCAR Research Forum 2016 Flyer.pdf 	" target="_blank"> </a></p>
<p><strong><strong><img src="https://paccar.unt.edu/sites/default/files/PACCAR Research Forum 2016 Flyer2.jpg" alt="" width="500" height="699" /></strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>January 20, 2016 - NASA, NOAA Announce 2015 Global Temperatures, Climate Conditions</strong></p>
<p>Climate experts from NASA and the National Oceanic and Atmospheric  Administration (NOAA) will discuss the release of new data on 2015  global temperatures, and the most important weather and climate events  of the year, during a media teleconference at 11 a.m. EST Wednesday,  Jan. 20.</p>
<p>The teleconference panelists are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Gavin Schmidt, director of NASA's Goddard Institute for Space Studies in New York</li>
<li>Thomas R. Karl, director of NOAA's National Centers for  Environmental Information in Asheville, North Carolina, and chair of the  Subcommittee on Global Change Research for the U.S. Global Change  Research Program in Washington</li>
</ul>
<p>Audio of the briefing, as well as supporting graphics, will stream live at:</p>
<p><a href="https://paccar.unt.edu/sites/default/files/PACCAR Research Forum 2016 Flyer.pdf 	" target="_blank"><strong>&nbsp;</strong></a><strong><a href="http://www.nasa.gov/newsaudio">http://www.nasa.gov/newsaudio</a></strong></p>
<p>NASA and NOAA are two keepers of the world's temperature data and  independently produce a record of Earth's surface temperatures, as well  as changes based on historical observations over oceans and land.</p>
<p>For more information about NASA's Earth science programs, visit:</p>
<p><a href="https://mail02.ndc.nasa.gov/owa/redir.aspx?SURL=SDgbuT5kuTL_kgeKzRx3RmSAVLv5v_RXbvSDoNgSbO_W7NJkmxrTCGgAdAB0AHAAOgAvAC8AdwB3AHcALgBuAGEAcwBhAC4AZwBvAHYALwBlAGEAcgB0AGgAcgBpAGcAaAB0AG4AbwB3AA..&amp;URL=http%3a%2f%2fwww.nasa.gov%2fearthrightnow"><strong>http://www.nasa.gov/earthrightnow</strong></a></p>
<p><strong>December 10, 2015 - DOE Post-Doctoral positions announced</strong></p>
<p>The US Department of Energy has recently announced the availability of 2 Post-Doctoral positions at the US Department of Energy, Building Technologies Office, Emerging Technologies Program:</p>
<p>* one position on analysis, simulation, and programming for energy policy</p>
<p>* one position on sensors &amp; controls for building energy efficiency</p>
<p>The detailed advertisement is available at <a href="https://www.zintellect.com/Posting/Details/1595">https://www.zintellect.com/Posting/Details/1595</a>.&nbsp; Applicants can learn more about the Emerging Technologies Program at <a href="http://energy.gov/eere/buildings/emerging-technologies">http://energy.gov/eere/buildings/emerging-technologies</a>.</p>
<p>Note that the location for both positions is DOE Headquarters in Washington DC, and that both positions are limited to US Citizens.&nbsp; The start date is negotiable, but no later than June, 2016.&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>September 11, 2015 - Mayor and City Council Business Awards Breakfast</strong></p>
<p>The Mayor and City Council Business Awards Breakfast was held on September 11, 2015 at UNT Apogee StadiumClub Level’s Hub Club.&nbsp; It serves as an opportunity for City departments, the Economic Development Partnership Board, Downtown Task Force and our higher education partners of UNT, TWU and NCTC to thank and recognize businesses with whom they have partnered in significant and beneficial ways.&nbsp; &nbsp;</p>
<p>This year, UNT President Neal Smatresk wishes to recognize Peterbilt Motors for its continuous support and collaborative efforts with UNT’s PACCAR Technology Institute, led by Dr. Yong X. Tao at Discovery Park in Department of Mechanical and Energy Engineering.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Peterbilt Motors has been recognized previously by the Economic Development Partnership Board in 2008, and Denton Municipal Electric in 2010.</p>
<p><img src="/sites/default/files/u6415/PACCAR%20Institute%202.jpg" alt="" width="640" height="401" /></p>
<p>From left:&nbsp; Mayor Chris Watts, Legislative Aid Dianne Helms, District Director for U S Congressman Erik With,General Manager of Peterbilt Darrin Siver, Chief Engineer of Peterbilt Scott Newhouse, UNT Dean of Engineering Costas Tsatsoulis, Director of Advanced Technology of Peterbilt Bill Khan, UNT President Neal Smatresk, &nbsp;Director of UNT’s PACCAR Technology Institute Yong Tao</p>
