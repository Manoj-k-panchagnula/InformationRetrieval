    <h3>Bachelor of Applied Arts and Sciences</h3>
    <p>The Bachelor of Applied Arts and Sciences (BAAS)
programs at the University of North Texas provide you a
direct path to a fully accredited bachelor’s degree if you have:</p>
		<ul>
        <li>Completed course work for technical, applied or
military credit</li>
		<li>A technical or applied degree from a community or
technical college</li>
		</ul>
        <p>Our BAAS degrees accept and build on these unique
credits, combining them with course work intended to
meet your individual or career goals.</p>
		<p>Flexible evening, weekend and online courses make it
easy for you to earn a BAAS degree while balancing
full-time or part-time work and your personal life.</p>
		<h4>Who benefits from a BAAS degree?</h4>
		<p>Students seeking admission to one of our BAAS
programs typically have some real-world work
experience. They also include:</p>
		<ul>
        <li>Active-duty military or veterans seeking college
credit for courses taken as part of their training in
the service</li>
		<li>Transfer students wanting credit for workforce or
technical courses taken at a community or technical
college</li>
		<li>Students interested in developing multiple areas of
expertise</li>
		<li>Students who’ve accumulated a significant number
of credit hours</li>
		</ul>
        <h4>What career opportunities are available?</h4>
		<p>Career opportunities for BAAS students vary as
much as their paths to earning the degree. Some students
complete the degree for career advancement. Others
want to change career paths altogether or ultimately plan
to attend graduate school. Whatever the motivation,
BAAS students graduate with an array of skills that
equip them to compete in today’s ever-changing
workplace.</p>
		<p>BAAS graduates are found in a variety of career fields.
Many work in business, state and local government,
nonprofit agencies or a wide range of fields directly associated
with the specialization they pursued at a community or
technical college or while in the military.</p>
		<p>UNT’s Career Center can provide advice about future
employment opportunities in a particular area.</p>
		<h4>What types of BAAS programs are offered?</h4>
		<p>We have two distinctive BAAS programs. One is
offered in Applied Arts and Sciences by the Department
of Community and Professional Programs in the College
of Public Affairs and Community Service. The other is in
Applied Technology and Performance Improvement through
the Department of Learning Technologies in the College
of Information. Each program has different requirements
and is designed to meet your specific needs or interests.</p>
		<h5>BAAS from the College of Public Affairs and Community Service</h5>
		<p>This multidisciplinary degree program builds on
your previous education and experience and allows you
to choose up to three areas of study that pertain to your
career interest and goals.</p>
		<p>You’re able to select courses from the various
disciplines offered at UNT. For example, you can take
courses in management, marketing, alternative dispute
resolution, nonprofit studies, criminal justice or a wide
variety of other disciplines. Our advisors work with you
to tailor a degree plan best suited for your needs.</p>
		<p>The program also gives you flexibility in how you
choose to study. Depending on the course, you can choose
to take classes online, face-to-face or in a blended format.
In blended-format courses, some instruction is provided
in face-to-face meetings with the remaining instruction
covered online.</p>
		<p>The specific degree requirements for this BAAS
program are outlined below.</p>
