<h3>How do we afford it?</h3>

<p>There are a number of options available to you and
your student to manage college costs. About 75 percent
of UNT students receive financial aid and scholarships
totaling $330 million annually. In addition to financial
aid and scholarships, options include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://studentaffairs.unt.edu/career-center">Student employment</a>: UNT employs 3,500
students through the Career Center’s Eagle Network.</li>
<li><a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/grants">Grants</a>: Many federal, state and institutional grants
are available at UNT. Grants are need-based and
no repayment is required.</li>
<li><a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/loans">Loans</a>: Loans are available at low interest rates
to students and parents of dependent students.</li>
<li><a href="http://studentaccounting.unt.edu/payments">Tuition and Fees</a>: UNT offers two tuition plans,
each with the option to pay in installments.
You also may be eligible for an exemption or waiver
available for veterans and their dependents.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Eagle Express Tuition Plan</h3>
<p>Tuition costs are a big part of planning for college.
UNT offers you an innovative solution to rising college costs with our <a href="https://eagleexpress.unt.edu/">Eagle Express Plan</a>, which is the most popular alternate tuition program in Texas. Eagle Express is
more than a tuition plan, it’s a plan for success.</p>
<ul>
<li>UNT’s Eagle Express plan locks in total
academic costs for four years.</li>
<li>It pays to graduate. Your student can save
up to $3,000 in their last 15 hours (typically the
last semester) by graduating in four years, and if
your student qualifies for the statewide $1,000 tuition rebate, the savings total $4,000.</li>
<li>Your student will save time and money by knowing
academic costs up front, graduating on time and
earning a full-time salary sooner.</li>
<li>More than half of incoming freshmen choose
the Eagle Express Tuition Plan, but a traditional
plan also is available.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Staying on track</h3>
<p>To help your student stay on track to graduate in four
years without taking extra courses, UNT offers an
online degree audit which lists all of your student’s
degree requirements in an easy-to-read format.
Learn more at the <a href="http://registrar.unt.edu">registrar's website</a>.</p>
<h3>Applying for financial aid</h3>
<p>You might be surprised to find out how much
financial aid you can receive, regardless of your
family’s income level. You won’t know what you’re
eligible for unless you apply! The best time to apply
for financial aid is in January of the year your student
plans to enroll. You must apply early to receive first
consideration for awards. The first step is completing
the <a href="http://fafsa.gov">Free Application for Federal Student Aid
(FAFSA)</a>.</p>

<h3>Scholarships</h3>
<p>UNT annually awards more than $39 million
in <a href="http://financialaid.unt.edu/scholarships">scholarships</a> to help finance student education.
The first step is to ensure your student is fully
admitted to UNT. Next, he or she will need to complete and submit the General Scholarship Application. Finally, make sure
you submit a FAFSA since many scholarships
require the application.</p>
<p>Your student may apply for scholarships funded
by the university, specific academic departments,
alumni and staff members. Merit-based, need-based
and athletic-based scholarships are usually available.
Examples include:</p>
<ul>
<li>UNT Meritorious Scholarships for National Merit Scholars</li>
<li>Terry Foundation Scholarships</li>
<li>UNT Excellence Scholarships</li>
<li>Academic Scholarships</li>
<li>Department-sponsored Scholarships</li>
</ul>

<h3>Financial success</h3>
<p>UNT’s <a href="https://studentaffairs.unt.edu/student-money-management-center">Student Money Management Center</a>
provides education and services to help
your student manage money and gain
financial independence.</p>

<p>Students learn how to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Manage credit</li>
<li>Analyze job offers and benefits packages</li>
<li>Live without Mom and Dad's financial support</li>
</ul>
