<h2>
	Mission</h2>

<p>
	The staff of the Coliseum will provide the highest quality customer service and maximum value in all that&nbsp;they do.</p>

<h2>
	History</h2>

<p>
	It has been called one of the finest basketball facilities in college athletics.&nbsp; The University of North Texas Coliseum, or &quot;The Super Pit,&quot; is home to the North Texas Men&#39;s and Women&#39;s basketball teams.&nbsp; Built in 1973, the Coliseum is designed for multipurpose use and has excellent viewing sight lines from any seat in the arena.</p>

<p>
	The arena, designed for functional flexibility, features aesthetic and technical excellence for both patrons and performers.&nbsp; The physical configuration evolved by combining the requirements of two diverse seating layouts; one for theatrical stage seating, and the other for coliseum-in-the-round seating.</p>

<p>
	A third major configuration may be constructed by locating a stage at one end of the arena floor.&nbsp; All of these major seating situations have been satisfied.&nbsp; The acoustics, lighting, speaker systems and various riggings are all suitable for the three configurations.</p>

<p>
	The Coliseum has 9,797 upholstered permanent theater type seats plus forty-eight positions for wheelchairs.&nbsp; With additional meeting space located on the lower level, the Coliseum is large enough to accommodate a wide range of activities.&nbsp; Besides basketball games, commencements and concerts, there have also been luncheons, workshops, dances, camps and competitions.&nbsp; Versatility is a key word in describing the Coliseum.</p>

<h2>
	Accessibility</h2>

<p>
	The east entrances to the Coliseum, located on Avenue D, are ground-level and allow easy access to the venue for our guests with special needs. In addition, there are limited handicapped parking spaces as well as a special needs drop-off area on this side of the Coliseum.</p>
