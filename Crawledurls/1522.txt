<h1>
	&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;Congratulations to the&nbsp;winners of the Union Arts Competition!</h1>

<p>
	<img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/union/staff/img_1690.jpg" style="width: 1045px; height: 783px;" /></p>

<p>
	&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; 1<sup>st</sup> place,&nbsp;$500: Given McClure</p>

<p>
	&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp;2<sup>nd</sup> place,&nbsp;$250: Julie Covington</p>

<p>
	&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp;3<sup>rd</sup> place, $100: Cameron Frazier</p>

<p>
	&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;</p>

<p>
	The Union Arts Competition had 75 entries total. Of those 75, 30 artists were selected by the Union Art Gallery Selection Committee, which is made up of faculty, staff, and students. The committee made the decision about which art would be included in the gallery and which pieces would win the cash prizes for 1st, 2nd, and 3rd place.&nbsp; &nbsp;<br />
	<br />
	The three winners represent totally different styles that represent the diversity and talent throughout our campus. First place winner, Given McClure, is a ceramics major who&rsquo;s graduating this semester. Given&rsquo;s piece, &ldquo;Meshes&rdquo; is a wall-hanging soft-sculpture. &ldquo;This work is designed to behave like a conversation with its viewer. Perspective is its focus and there are no real answers, only questions to ask yourself&rdquo; McClure said about his competition piece. &ldquo;The Union presented a challenging yet very engaging setting for a kinetic sculpture.&rdquo; Given&rsquo;s work has also recently been on display in his own show, which took place at Voertman&rsquo;s on April 27th.</p>

<p>
	Second place winner Julie Covington is studying photography. Her piece, titled &ldquo;Ophelia&rdquo; is a set of Giclee prints. Julie said she &ldquo;love[s] film photography the most. There&rsquo;s something about the depth that film gives you that digital just can&rsquo;t do.&rdquo; She chose to submit her piece because she thought that people would understand her reference to Shakespeare&rsquo;s Ophelia. In the meantime, Julie is interested in &ldquo;3D sculptures printed from 3d modeling and scans.&rdquo;</p>

<p>
	Third place winner Cameron Frazier is a sophomore double major in Communication Design and Business Marketing. He came to UNT because of its great Communication Design program and its proximity to his hometown of Rockwall, Texas. His piece &ldquo;Crow&rsquo;s Nest&rdquo; is a pen ink drawing. &ldquo;I enjoy working with pen on fine detail realism pieces the most. I actually specifically made the piece for this competition. I&#39;d had the idea in mind for about six months, but never had the time or motivation to work on it, as I knew it&#39;d be much more work than my usual pieces. This competition gave me the drive to actually complete the idea!&rdquo; said Frazier. He is also working on a concept right now that will include his competition piece. &ldquo;The series is war photography from the apocalypse,&rdquo; Frazier said.</p>

<p>
	The Union&rsquo;s new Art Gallery has posed an exciting new space for artists at UNT to display their work. Congratulations to the three winners and the 30 artists who were featured.</p>
