	      <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded"> <p>You're almost there, graduate! Taking part in commencement is the ceremonial celebration when you can share in the excitement and joy of becoming a UNT graduate with your family and friends. We can't wait to celebrate with you.</p>
<p>Learn more below about the steps to <a href="http://registrar.unt.edu/graduation-and-diplomas/information-for-graduates">apply for graduation</a> and to <a href="../information-spring-graduates#participating">participate in commencement events</a>.</p>
<h3>Distinction between Graduating and Participating in Commencement</h3>
<p>Commencement events and recognition ceremonies are the congratulatory ceremonies hosted by university officials in which students are publicly and formally recognized.</p>
<p>Graduation is the process of being awarded a degree after successfully completing credit hours and other degree requirements. Actual diplomas are not awarded at commencement ceremonies and participation in the ceremonies is not a guarantee of degree completion. To officially graduate from UNT, a student must apply for graduation by the posted deadline. After receipt of final grades, the student's degree audit must show the student satisfied all university, college and departmental requirements for the specific degree. A degree becomes official when it is posted to a student's transcript -- typically two to three weeks after the completion of their final term -- not by their participation in commencement exercises. A student does not have to be present at a commencement ceremony to receive their degree.</p>
<p>Advising is an important part of making sure you're on track to graduate and that you understand when you can expect to graduate and participate in commencement. <a href="http://advising.unt.edu/">Learn more about meeting with an advisor</a><strong>.</strong></p>
<h3>Graduation Information</h3>
<p>Students who plan to complete degree requirements in May must apply for spring graduation. The Registrar's Office website includes all <a href="https://registrar.unt.edu/graduation-and-diplomas/apply-for-graduation">application instructions and deadlines</a>.</p>
<ul><li>Login to <a href="https://my.unt.edu/psp/papd01/EMPLOYEE/EMPL/h/?tab=NTPA_GUEST">MyUNT</a> and from the Student Center click on the “Apply for Graduation” link in the Other Academic drop down. The “Apply for Graduation” link will be visible, even if you may not be eligible for graduation at this time. Please see your academic advising office for more information.</li>
</ul><p>Graduation applicants will be mailed commencement information packets approximately two weeks prior to their commencement ceremony. The information packets mailed to graduation applicants will include:</p>
<ul><li>Links to RSVP for the ceremonies. RSVPs must be received by the deadline listed in the packet.</li>
<li>Official commencement name cards</li>
<li>Detailed instructions that are specific to each student and the ceremony or ceremonies they will attend.</li>
</ul><h4><a name="participating" id="participating"></a>University-wide Commencement</h4>
<ul><li><strong>What is it:</strong> The annual University-wide Commencement features traditional commencement elements such as a processional of graduation candidates into Apogee Stadium and a conferral of degrees by the president when students stand and are formally recognized by group (bachelor's, master's and doctoral graduates). Commencement also features an inspirational speaker. <a href="../information-spring-graduates#regalia">Graduates wear their regalia at this event</a>.</li>
</ul><ul><li><strong>Who can participate: </strong>All UNT graduates from the current academic year may participate in the Spring University-wide Commencement.</li>
</ul><p><a href="../spring-commencement-overview">Learn more about Spring ceremonies</a>.</p>
<ul><li><strong>When is it: </strong>University-wide Commencement is held every May. <strong>The Spring 2016 University-wide Commencement is May 13, 2016. </strong></li>
</ul><ul><li><strong>Who can attend:</strong> Graduates are encouraged to invite family and friends. There is no limit to the number of guests that graduates can invite to attend the University-wide Commencement. Graduates and their guests are expected to remain for the duration of the event.</li>
</ul><h4>Spring Undergraduate College Recognition Ceremony</h4>
<ul><li><strong>What is it: </strong>Each college holds Undergraduate Recognition Ceremonies in May for bachelor's degree candidates that feature traditional commencement elements in a more intimate setting. Candidates are called by name , walk the stage, shake hands with their college administration, and get their <a href="../information-spring-graduates#photo">photo taken</a>. Candidates wear their <a href="../information-spring-graduates#regalia">regalia</a> at this event<strong>.</strong> Because these candidates will have their degrees conferred at the University-wide Commencement, the spring college recognition ceremonies do not include degree conferral.</li>
</ul><ul><li><strong>Who can participate: </strong>If you graduate in May, you can participate in your college’s Undergraduate College Recognition Ceremony.
<ul><li>To participate in a Spring Undergraduate College Recognition Ceremony, a candidate for May graduation must apply for graduation by the posted deadline, receive advisor approval on their graduation application, and must RSVP that they plan to attend the ceremony. <a href="http://registrar.unt.edu/graduation-and-diplomas/information-for-graduates">Learn more about applying for graduation</a></li>
<li>Undergraduate students who are planning to complete degree requirements during summer may receive special permission to participate in their college’s spring recognition ceremony. Interested candidates must complete their application for summer graduation by the posted spring graduation application deadline. These candidates must also must receive advisor approval and must RSVP to attend the ceremony. <a href="http://registrar.unt.edu/graduation-and-diplomas/information-for-graduates">Learn more about applying for graduation</a>. </li>
</ul></li>
</ul><ul><li><strong>When is it: </strong>Undergraduate College Recognition Ceremonies are held every May. <strong>The Spring 2016 Undergraduate College Recognition Ceremonies will be held May 13-14, 2016. </strong><a href="../spring-schedule">Check the schedule</a>.</li>
</ul><ul><li><strong>Who can attend: </strong>Candidates can invite family and friends. Candidates and their guests are expected to remain for the duration of the ceremony.</li>
</ul><h4><a name="doctoral" id="doctoral"></a>Spring Doctoral and Master's Convocation with Doctoral/M.F.A. Hooding</h4>
<ul><li><strong>What is it:</strong> The university holds a separate ceremony in May for master's and doctoral candidates that features traditional commencement elements and recognizes their unique accomplishments. Master’s candidates process to the stage, have their name called, walk the stage and shake hands with university administration, and have their <a href="../information-spring-graduates#photo">photo taken</a>. Doctoral and M.F.A. candidates (recipients of terminal degrees) are hooded during this ceremony by their faculty mentor(s). Candidates wear their <a href="../information-spring-graduates#regalia">regalia</a> at this event. Because degree conferral happens at the University-wide Commencement, the spring doctoral and Master’s convocation does not include degree conferral.</li>
</ul><ul><li><strong>Who can participate</strong>: Doctoral and master's students earning degrees in May will be recognized in the spring Doctoral and Master's Convocation. To participate in the spring <a href="../spring-commencement-overview#doctoral">Doctoral and Master's Convocation</a>, doctoral and master's candidates must apply for graduation by the posted deadline and RSVP using the links provided in the mailed graduation packet.<br /><ul style="list-style-type:circle"><li>Master's candidates must have completed all degree requirements in May to participate in the spring ceremony.</li>
<li>Doctoral candidates must have defended their dissertation by April 15th to participate in the spring ceremony.</li>
</ul></li>
</ul><ul><li><strong>Who can attend: </strong>Graduates can invite family and friends. Graduates and their guests are expected to remain for the duration of the ceremony.</li>
</ul><h3>Commencement Events Details</h3>
<h4>Graduation Packets</h4>
<p>Graduation packets mailed to candidates approximately two weeks prior to their ceremony contain important instructions that candidates will need to follow before and during the events.</p>
<ul><li><a href="../spring-commencement-overview">Learn more about the different events</a>.</li>
<li>To find out when and where your event will be, check the <a href="../spring-schedule">Spring 2016 commencement schedule</a>.</li>
</ul><h4>Line-up and Recognition</h4>
<p><strong>University-wide Commencement</strong></p>
<ul><li>For the University-wide Commencement, candidates should arrive one hour before the event starts.</li>
<li>Graduates will start lining up at 9:15 a.m. and the graduate processional will begin at 9:45 a.m.</li>
<li>Candidates will not be lined up in particular order and they do not need to bring name cards.</li>
<li>Candidates will take part in a formal processional into Apogee Stadium and will be seated as directed by commencement marshals on the stadium field.</li>
<li>The president will confer degrees by asking students to stand and be formally recognized by group (bachelor's, master's and doctoral candidates).</li>
<li>At the close of the ceremony, candidates will be directed out of the stadium.</li>
</ul><p><strong>Spring Undergraduate College Recognition Ceremony </strong></p>
<ul><li>Line-up for the Undergraduate College Recognition Ceremonies will begin one hour prior to the event in the respective venue. There will be signage directing candidates where to line up at each event.</li>
<li>Candidates will process into the respective venue and be seated as directed by commencement marshals.</li>
<li>Candidates will be called to the stage by name for individual recognition so they need to bring the name card that was included in their information packet. The name card will also indicate the student's position in the line-up.</li>
<li>Candidates should watch for cues from marshals who will indicate when and where candidates should approach the stage.</li>
<li>Candidates will walk up the stairs and hand their name cards to the announcer before they walk onto the stage. Wheelchair lifts or ramps will be available for those with mobility concerns. Candidates should make note of this need on their RSVP information.</li>
</ul><p><strong>Spring Doctoral and Master’s Convocation</strong></p>
<ul><li>Line-up for the Doctoral and Master’s Convocation will begin one hour before the event starts.</li>
<li>Candidates will take part in a formal processional into the venue and will be seated as directed by commencement marshals.</li>
<li>Candidates will be called to the stage by name for individual recognition so they need to bring the name card that was included in their information packet. The name card will indicate the student's position in the line-up.</li>
<li>Candidates should watch for cues from marshals who will indicate when and where candidates should approach the stage.Candidates will walk up the stairs and hand their name cards to the announcer before they walk onto the stage. Wheelchair lifts or ramps will be available for those with mobility concerns. Candidates should make note of this need on their RSVP information.</li>
<li>Doctoral and M.F.A. candidates (recipients of terminal degrees) will be hooded by their mentor professor(s).</li>
</ul><h3><a name="jazz" id="jazz"></a>Jazz on the Hill</h3>
<ul><li>Festivities begin at 7 a.m. Friday, May 13, on the Hill next to Apogee Stadium</li>
<li>Complimentary morning refreshments is available for graduates and guests</li>
<li>Stadium doors open at 7 a.m.</li>
</ul><p><a href="../spring-commencement-overview#jazz">Learn more about Jazz on the Hill</a></p>
<h3><a name="regalia" id="regalia"></a>Regalia</h3>
<p>Graduates will wear <a href="../regalia-overview">official approved regalia</a> for all formal commencement events. All arrangements for regalia approved by the university should be made with the official provider, <a href="http://unt.bncollege.com/">Barnes and Noble at UNT</a>, located inside the University Union, 1155 Union Cir, Denton, TX, 76203 (940-565-2592). Master’s and bachelor’s candidates will receive a tassel as part of their regalia purchase or rental.</p>
<h3><a name="attire" id="attire"></a>Attire</h3>
<p>Women should wear cool, dark dresses, skirts or slacks and dark shoes. Men should wear dress shirts, dark trousers, a tie and dark shoes. Graduates will be more comfortable in cool fabrics and comfortable shoes. Please do not bring handbags, umbrellas, extra shoes or coats as there will not be a place to store them during the ceremony. <strong>You may carry in your cell phone and keys</strong> but during the Undergraduate College Recognition Ceremonies and the Doctoral and Master’s Convocation, there will not be a secure place to leave these items as you cross the stage. If you choose to bring these items into the ceremony, you must leave them at your own risk in your seat while you cross the stage.</p>
<h3>Guests</h3>
<p>UNT does not issue or require tickets for the University-wide Commencement and there is no limit to the number of guests that graduates can invite to this event. Guests will be seated in the stadium seats at Apogee Stadium.</p>
<p>For the college ceremonies and doctoral and master’s convocation, graduates can invite family and friends.</p>
<h3><a name="photo" id="photo"></a>Photo</h3>
<p>Graduates will have their photo taken by professional photographers during the Undergraduate College Recognition Ceremonies and the Doctoral and Master's Convocation. A posed photo will be taken with your diploma cover, and a candid shot will be taken as you shake hands with university officials. Proofs of these photos will be emailed from Flash Photography to the graduate’s UNT email address in the weeks following the ceremony.</p>
