          <h2 class="header-title">Materials Science and Engineering Professor Elected to AIMBE College of Fellows</h2>
<!--<style>

#news-image {float: right;}

</style>-->

<div id="node-2812" class="node clear-block">


<p></p>

  <div class="content">

  	<div class="news-byline">

		<p><span class="date-display-single">April 2016</span><br />

                <br />

        by: UNT College of Engineering</p>

    </div>

    <div id="news-image">

        <img src="http://engineering.unt.edu/sites/default/files/imagecache/news_image_large/Dahotre_AIMBE_award.jpg" alt="" title="" width="263" height="400" class="imagecache imagecache-news_image_large imagecache-default imagecache-news_image_large_default"/>
    </div>

    <div class="news-body">

        <p><p>Dr.&nbsp;<a href="http://engineering.unt.edu/materials/dr-narendra-dahotre">Narendra Dahotre</a>, a Distinguished Research Professor of the&nbsp;<a href="http://engineering.unt.edu/materials/">Department of Materials Science and Engineering</a>, has been elected to the&nbsp;<a href="http://aimbe.org/" target="_blank">American Institute for Medical and Biological Engineering</a>&nbsp;(AIMBE) College of Fellows.</p>
<p>The AIMBE College of Fellows represents the most accomplished and distinguished medical and biological engineers responsible for innovation and discovery. Since AIMBE’s inception, more than 1,500 individuals have been inducted into AIMBE’s College of Fellows. According to an&nbsp;<a href="http://www.aimbe.org/press/Dahotre-COF-1939.pdf" target="_blank">AIMBE press release</a>, Dahotre was nominated, reviewed, and elected by peers and members of the College of Fellows for pioneering contributions to fundamentals and engineering of laser-material interactions during advanced manufacturing and processing of biomaterials and sustained service to the materials community.</p>
<p><span>A formal induction ceremony was held during AIMBE’s 25th Annual Meeting at the National Academy of Sciences Great Hall in Washington, DC on April 4, 2016. Dahotre was inducted along with other colleagues who make up the AIMBE College of Fellows Class of 2016.</span></p>
<p>Dahotre has devoted his educational and professional career to developing laser-based surface engineering for advanced materials. He has made pioneering contributions to understanding and engineering laser-materials interactions and implementation of high power lasers for materials processing and surface engineering.&nbsp; Dahotre is internationally known for his work on fundamentals and applications of laser surface engineering of metals, ceramics, polymers and composites.&nbsp; He has published and edited more than 250 technical papers, written 4 books and 5 book chapters in his career and also holds 16 patents. Several of his patents have been licensed for commercial applications including a recently granted (March 17, 2016) US Patent <strong><em>‘Laser-assisted Machining (LAM) of Hard Tissues and Bones’</em></strong><em> (#</em>US 20140263214) to the Australian Institute of Robotic Orthopaedics.</p></p>
