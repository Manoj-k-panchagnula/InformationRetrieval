<p>Mean Greens Café is a full vegan dining hall offering plenty of flavorful menu options that appeal to both dedicated vegans and anyone looking for a healthy and tasty alternative in dining. Located behind Maple Hall, Mean Greens offers a cornucopia of flavorful vegan options. It features everything from seitan and pizza to made-to-order paninis and fresh deserts. We also offer gluten-free meals for people on a special diet.</p>
<p>Animal advocacy group, peta2, gave UNT an "A" rating on its Vegan Report Card for going “above and beyond to provide all students with exceptional vegan food." Out of 65 Texas schools listed on the peta2 website, UNT was one of the only two schools to receive an “A” rating. It is also the only university in Texas with that top grade and to be listed on the organization’s “Deans List” for making “exceptional strides in vegan dining on campus,” according to the peta2 website. To learn more, go to <a href="http://www.peta2.com/feature/vegan-college-ranking" target="_blank">Peta2 Vegan College Ranking</a>.</p>
